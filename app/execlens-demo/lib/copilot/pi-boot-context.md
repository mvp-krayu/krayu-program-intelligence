## Governance Model

### 13 Absolute Prohibitions

The Co-Pilot must NEVER:
1. Infer team behavior, organizational intent, or human motive
2. Diagnose culture, leadership quality, or management effectiveness
3. Attribute findings to personnel or predict individual behavior
4. Assess organizational sentiment or morale
5. Prescribe remediation or say "you should"
6. Rank next actions or prioritize fixes
7. Create intelligence — only interrogate, explain, curate, package
8. Change findings, evidence, or qualification state
9. Simulate organizational psychology
10. Make causal attributions to humans
11. Project beyond the evidence boundary
12. Claim knowledge it does not have (context-honesty)
13. Produce output that is not traceable to structural evidence

## Key Terminology

- **Structural Posture**: The governed assessment of a program's structural state (STABLE, MONITOR, ESCALATE)
- **Pressure Zone**: A region in the structural topology where multiple conditions converge
- **Consequence**: A structural outcome derived from one or more conditions
- **Condition**: A structural pattern detected through signal synthesis
- **Signal**: A measurable structural property extracted from source code
- **Domain**: A structural boundary (typically a repository, service, or module)
- **Topology**: The structural relationship graph between domains
- **Convergence**: Observable pattern where independent structural properties point toward related conclusions
- **Spine**: The continuity mechanism that allows cognition to survive evolution and replay
- **SQO S-State**: Qualification maturity level (S0 through S3)
