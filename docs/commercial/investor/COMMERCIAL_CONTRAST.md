## The Current State of Technology Assessment

High-value technology intelligence activities are delivered today through expert-dependent, manual processes. These engagements are expensive, slow, subjective, and non-repeatable.

---

## Today vs. Program Intelligence

| Activity | Today | Timeline | Program Intelligence | Timeline |
|----------|-------|----------|---------------------|----------|
| **Technology Due Diligence** | Consulting team. Manual code review, architecture interviews, stakeholder sessions. Findings depend on which consultants are assigned. | 4–8 weeks | Governed structural assessment from code evidence. Deterministic — same evidence produces same findings. Full evidence lineage. | Hours to days |
| **Architecture Assessment** | Senior architect review. Workshop-based. Conclusions depend on experience and access. No systematic structural analysis. | 2–6 weeks | Runtime-qualified structural topology. Every finding traces to specific files, dependencies, and structural patterns. | Hours to days |
| **Portfolio Technology Review** | Point-in-time consulting engagement per asset. Cannot be updated without re-engaging consultants. No cross-portfolio structural comparison. | Weeks per asset | Each asset produces a governed PICP. Cross-portfolio structural comparison is a runtime operation, not a new engagement. | Days per asset, continuous updates |
| **Transformation Readiness** | Manual expert evaluation. Methodology varies by firm and engagement. No evidence traceability for readiness conclusions. | 3–8 weeks | Evidence-qualified structural assessment of transformation feasibility. Explicit qualification of what the evidence supports and where structural blind spots exist. | Days |
| **M&A Technical Due Diligence** | Third-party consulting. High cost, variable quality, tight timeline pressure. Findings cannot be independently replayed or verified. | 4–12 weeks | Governed assessment with full replay capability. A second run produces identical findings. Evidence lineage enables independent verification. | Days |
| **Delivery Risk Assessment** | Program manager judgment. Status reports. Earned-value calculations on self-reported data. No structural evidence of execution ceiling. | Ongoing, manual | Structural analysis of program execution constraints — dependency bottlenecks, propagation asymmetry, coupling pressure. Evidence-based, not opinion-based. | Hours to days |

## What Changes

| Dimension | Consulting-Led | Program Intelligence |
|-----------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Speed** | Weeks to months | Hours to days |
| **Cost** | High — expert-dependent | Lower — runtime-driven with expert interpretation |
| **Repeatability** | Low — different team, different result | High — same evidence, same findings |
| **Evidence Traceability** | Minimal — conclusions in slide decks | Complete — every finding traces to structural evidence |
| **Replayability** | None — engagement is a point-in-time event | Full — any finding can be reproduced from inputs |
| **Continuous Updates** | Requires new engagement | Runtime re-processes updated evidence |
| **Scalability** | Linear — more assets require more consultants | Multiplicative — more evidence sources and consumers compound |
| **Qualification** | Implicit — based on consultant reputation | Explicit — S-Level, Q-Class, authority ceiling, governance metadata |

---

## What Program Intelligence Does Not Replace

Program Intelligence does not replace strategic advisors, transformation leaders, or executive judgment.

It replaces the **evidence foundation** they depend on — the weeks of manual analysis, the subjective structural interpretation, the non-traceable conclusions, and the non-repeatable methodology.

The expert conversation shifts from: *"Here is what we found after six weeks"*
to: *"Here is what the governed runtime found in hours — let's discuss what it means for your decision."*

The intelligence becomes industrialized. The advisory conversation becomes higher-value.
