# Signäl LENS — Commercial Plan v1

## Executive Audience: CTO + Chief of Marketing + Graphics Designer

---

## PART 1 — DIAGNOSIS

The current website has three failure modes:

**1. No hook.**
The page opens with a product name and a description. There is no moment where the reader recognizes their own problem. Executives do not buy tools. They buy resolution of a situation they are already in.

**2. No proof of depth.**
The product is structurally interesting — topology views, evidence chains, trace interrogation, score confidence bands. None of this is visible. The website reads like a brochure for a service that might exist.

**3. No journey.**
There is no ladder. A reader cannot tell what they get now, what they need to unlock, and where this leads. The product has a clear three-tier architecture (Assessment → Diagnostic → Enterprise). The website does not.

### Section 3 — THE PRODUCT LADDER

Three-tier presentation. Left to right.

**Tier 1 — LENS Assessment**
- Tag: Included
- What you get: Executive structural view. 17-domain system intelligence. Structural topology. Focus domain spotlight. Decision guidance.
- When to use: First engagement. Pre-transformation clarity. Scoping for investment decisions.

**Tier 2 — Diagnostic Access**
- Tag: Entitlement Required
- What you get: Everything in LENS + trace interrogation, capability-level topology, evidence chain validation, operational detail (L2), full audit trail (L3).
- When to use: When the assessment identifies a zone that requires deeper investigation.

**Tier 3 — Enterprise Access**
- Tag: Governed Contract
- What you get: Full operator surface. Direct evidence vault access. Program-level intelligence queries. Integration planning support.
- When to use: Continuous structural intelligence at program or organization scale.

**Ladder logic copy:**
> The Assessment tells you where to look.  
> The Diagnostic tells you what is there.  
> Enterprise access keeps you informed continuously.

### Section 4 — INSIDE AN ASSESSMENT

Four steps. Numbered. No bullets.

1. **Intake** — A lightweight, non-intrusive collection of relevant context from your environment. No instrumentation. No agent installation. No extended access required.

2. **Analysis** — Transformation of existing information into a structured representation. The environment is mapped: domains, connections, pressure zones, confidence bands.

3. **Report** — Generation of the executive LENS view. A structured, print-ready artifact designed for leadership use. Not a slide deck. Not a status summary. A structural view.

4. **Review** — A guided session to interpret findings, contextualize the topology, and define next steps. The review produces a clear decision: continue at this depth, or go deeper.

### Section 6 — TRIGGER CARDS

Six situations. Short cards. When a reader recognizes one, they are the buyer.

1. **Pre-transformation** — Before committing to a major organizational or technology transformation, you need to know what you are actually working with. Not what teams report. What the structure reveals.

2. **Recurring friction** — The same delivery problems keep appearing in different places. No one can explain why. LENS finds the structural source.

3. **Scaling pressure** — The organization is growing. Execution is getting harder to see. You need a structural view before you scale into opacity.

4. **Investment decision** — You are about to commit significant capital to a program or a vendor. You need independent structural clarity before the decision is made.

5. **Post-incident** — Something failed. The immediate cause is addressed. The structural driver is not. LENS finds what the incident report does not say.

6. **New leadership** — You have inherited a complex environment. You need to understand it before you change it.

## PART 4 — VISUAL ASSETS

Six assets. Described for design production.

---

### Asset 01 — Topology Hero Image

**Purpose:** Homepage hero. First visual impression.

**Description:**
A node-graph rendered in a dark field. Clusters of interconnected nodes — some bright green (verified), some amber (conditional), some blue (focus). Edges between clusters. The graph is clearly structured — not random — but shows visible concentration in one cluster. Clean, high-contrast. No labels visible in the hero version. A structural impression, not a data dump.

**Usage:** Full-width hero behind the Section 1 headline.

**Tone:** Technical depth, executive restraint. Not a startup illustration. Not a dashboard screenshot. A structural artifact.

---

### Asset 02 — Score Band Diagram

**Purpose:** Explain confidence range concept without jargon.

**Description:**
Horizontal band diagram. A central score (e.g. 68) with a shaded range band (e.g. 58–78). Below: a label "CONFIDENCE RANGE" with a brief explanation: "Score is anchored to evidence. Range reflects unresolved depth." Three zones: green (high confidence), amber (moderate), red (low confidence / contested). The score sits in the amber zone in this example.

**Usage:** Section 5 (The Report). Inline with report component list.

**Tone:** Precise. Clinical. Not decorative.

---

### Asset 03 — Depth Ladder Cross-Section

**Purpose:** Explain the three-tier product ladder visually.

**Description:**
A vertical cross-section diagram. Three horizontal layers, labeled:
- L1 — LENS Assessment (top layer, accessible)
- L2/L3 — Diagnostic Access (middle layer, gated — shown with a lock marker at the boundary)
- L4 — Enterprise / Vault (bottom layer, deeper gating)

Vertical axis label: "Intelligence Depth". Each layer shows what is visible at that tier. The boundary between L1 and L2 is marked with a visual gate element.

**Usage:** Section 3 (Product Ladder). Full-width between ladder tier cards.

**Tone:** Engineering diagram aesthetic. Monochrome with single accent color per tier.

---

### Asset 04 — Before / After Structural View

**Purpose:** Show the transformation LENS produces.

**Description:**
Two-panel layout. Left panel: "Before LENS" — fragmented view. Multiple disconnected boxes labeled "Status Report", "Dashboard", "Team Metric", "Incident Log". No connections. Gaps between boxes. Label: "Activity visibility. No structural view."

Right panel: "After LENS" — same elements, now connected into a coherent topology. Edges show relationships. Cluster shapes visible. One node highlighted in amber (pressure zone identified). Label: "Structural visibility. Pressure zones located."

**Usage:** Section 2 (What LENS Reveals) or Section 4 (Inside an Assessment).

**Tone:** Simple. Clear contrast. Executive-legible in under 5 seconds.

---

### Asset 05 — Report Page Preview

**Purpose:** Show the report is real. Show what it looks like.

**Description:**
A rendered preview of the LENS Executive Report — a single page, slightly angled (perspective tilt for premium feel). Visible elements: score band at top, section headers, domain list in 2-column layout, a small topology view in the corner. Text is legible but not the focus — the artifact presence is.

**Usage:** Section 5 (The Report). Full-width or right-aligned beside copy.

**Tone:** High-quality document. Not a slide. Not a web screenshot. A physical artifact rendered digitally.

---

### Asset 06 — Gate Moment

**Purpose:** Explain the gating concept without friction. Make restriction feel like value.

**Description:**
A single UI moment: the LENS intelligence surface, with a blurred/frosted panel behind a modal. The modal headline: "Secure Access Required". Below it: three capability lines (trace interrogation, evidence chain validation, full audit trail) — each with a lock icon. CTA: "Unlock Access". The blur implies depth behind the gate. The gate implies there is something worth protecting.

**Usage:** Inline with Section 3 (Product Ladder) or on a standalone Diagnostic Access landing page.

**Tone:** Premium access management. Not a paywall. An entitlement moment.
