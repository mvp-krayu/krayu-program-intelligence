
# 30-Day WoW Demonstration
Krayu — Program Intelligence Platform
Stream 50 — Program Intelligence Demonstrations

## Purpose

The 30-Day WoW Demonstration is a controlled Program Intelligence environment designed to show how execution telemetry can reveal meaningful program signals within a short observation window.

The goal of this demonstration is to provide observable proof that Program Intelligence can transform operational execution data into interpretable signals and executive insight within a 30-day analytical cycle.

This demonstration serves as a proof environment for the Program Intelligence discipline and provides a concrete illustration of the analytical chain:

Execution Evidence
→ Signal Detection
→ Program Intelligence
→ Executive Insight

---

## Demonstration Scenario

The demonstration simulates a delivery program operating across multiple delivery teams executing iterative work cycles.

The simulated environment includes:

• backlog items representing delivery commitments  
• iteration cycles representing execution cadence  
• work item lifecycle transitions  
• delivery progress signals  
• backlog movement patterns

The demonstration environment reproduces realistic program execution conditions where delivery behavior can be observed and analyzed.

---

## Execution Context

The execution context represents a program with the following characteristics:

Program Structure

• multiple delivery teams  
• iterative execution cycles  
• shared backlog environment  
• interdependent work items

Execution Cadence

• short iteration cycles  
• regular delivery commitments  
• continuous backlog evolution

The context is designed to produce observable execution telemetry within the 30‑day observation window.

---

## Evidence Sources

Evidence used in the demonstration may include:

• work item lifecycle history  
• backlog additions and removals  
• iteration commitments and completions  
• work item aging patterns  
• work distribution across teams

Evidence must represent observable execution activity.

All signals generated in the demonstration must be traceable to these evidence sources.

---

## Signals Generated

Signals represent detectable patterns in execution behavior.

Example signals that may emerge during the demonstration include:

• delivery acceleration signals  
• delivery deceleration signals  
• backlog instability signals  
• delivery concentration signals  
• execution volatility signals

Signals must originate from observable evidence patterns rather than hypothetical assumptions.

---

## Program Intelligence Outputs

Program Intelligence interprets signals to produce insight about the state of the program.

The demonstration may reveal:

• delivery momentum changes  
• backlog instability conditions  
• emerging execution bottlenecks  
• concentration of delivery workload  
• increasing execution volatility

These insights represent analytical interpretations of signal patterns.

---

## Executive Narrative

The demonstration must ultimately produce an executive‑level interpretation of the observed signals.

This narrative may address questions such as:

• Is the program execution trajectory improving or deteriorating?
• Are delivery patterns stable or unstable?
• Are emerging risks visible in execution behavior?
• Where should leadership focus attention?

Executive insight must always remain grounded in the evidence and signals generated during the demonstration.

---

## Demonstration Outcome

The outcome of the 30-Day WoW Demonstration is a visible illustration of how Program Intelligence reveals meaningful execution insight from operational telemetry.

The demonstration shows that within a short observation window it is possible to identify patterns that indicate program execution conditions and emerging delivery risks.

This demonstration provides observable validation that the Program Intelligence discipline can transform execution data into interpretable intelligence.
