## 0. WHAT THIS DOCUMENT IS

This document is the doctrine-level summary of the cinematic reference families that bound LENS visual direction.

It exists for two reasons:

1. To make the bounding reference set explicit inside the doctrine package itself, so any reader of the doctrine encounters it without needing to chase to a sister stream.
2. To enforce that future LENS visual contracts are evaluated against an explicit reference set, not against vague taste.

Detailed deep-dives for each reference family live in:

> `docs/psee/PI.LENS.V2.CINEMATIC-REFERENCE-MAPPING.01/`

If the deep-dive document for a reference family disagrees with this summary, the deep-dive wins (it is the more specific document).

## 1. WHY REFERENCES MATTER

Without explicit comparative grounding, all visual implementation converges on the same generic developer-designed dark dashboard. This is not a stylistic preference; it is an empirical observation about what un-anchored design produces by default.

References serve as:

- **Calibration** — what "premium operational" looks like at world-class level.
- **Constraint** — what LENS may borrow and what LENS must not borrow.
- **Comparison** — concrete evaluation targets for inspection passes.

A reference is **not**:

- A template to copy.
- A brand to imitate.
- A look to match pixel-for-pixel.

A reference is *what level of seriousness, restraint, or atmosphere LENS is being held to*.

## 2. THE BOUNDING REFERENCE FAMILIES

There are nine reference families. They are bounding — meaning together they define a region of visual space inside which LENS may live, and outside which LENS may not.

### 2.1 Operational Intelligence — Palantir Gotham / Foundry

**Borrow:** operational gravity, investigative seriousness, evidence-first atmosphere, command-center realism.
**Avoid:** military intimidation aesthetic, cluttered intelligence overload, overly tactical visual aggression.
**Why it matters to LENS:** establishes the ceiling for "this is operational, not analytical."

### 2.2 Expert Density — Bloomberg Terminal

**Borrow:** confidence through clarity, serious operational energy, information-hierarchy discipline.
**Avoid:** legacy terminal ugliness, overwhelming textual compression, expert-only inaccessibility.
**Why it matters to LENS:** proves that density and seriousness can coexist.

### 2.3 Spatial Hierarchy — Apple Vision Pro / visionOS

**Borrow:** spatial hierarchy, cinematic restraint, depth orchestration, atmospheric composure.
**Avoid:** floating gimmick UI, sci-fi fantasy effects, excessive translucency.
**Why it matters to LENS:** the modern reference for restrained spatial layering.

### 2.4 Premium Calm — Linear

**Borrow:** calm operational confidence, premium restraint, motion subtlety, typography discipline.
**Avoid:** startup productivity energy, generic SaaS aesthetics, issue-tracker feel.
**Why it matters to LENS:** the modern reference for disciplined density without dashboard syndrome.

### 2.5 Command Surface — Raycast

**Borrow:** command confidence, restrained UI chrome, premium utility feel.
**Avoid:** utility-tool flatness, developer-only ergonomics.
**Why it matters to LENS:** the modern reference for "I know exactly what this is for" without dashboard scaffolding.

### 2.6 Atmospheric Workspace — Arc Browser

**Borrow:** atmospheric continuity, environmental immersion, product personality.
**Avoid:** playful experimentation, consumer-product energy.
**Why it matters to LENS:** the modern reference for atmospheric depth that doesn't tip into decoration.

### 2.7 Presentation Quality — Stripe / Vercel

**Borrow:** presentation precision, visual confidence, disciplined typography systems.
**Avoid:** marketing-page energy, startup-optimism aesthetics.
**Why it matters to LENS:** the modern reference for institutional-grade visual finish.

### 2.8 Mission Operations — NASA / SpaceX operational environments

**Borrow:** operational realism, calm consequence management, mission-grade seriousness.
**Avoid:** retro control-room nostalgia, overloaded telemetry chaos.
**Why it matters to LENS:** the upper bound for "consequence is real, the surface acts like it knows."

### 2.9 Editorial Hierarchy — Financial Times / institutional briefings

**Borrow:** editorial hierarchy, executive reading comfort, narrative pacing.
**Avoid:** newspaper density, traditional publishing rigidity.
**Why it matters to LENS:** the reference for how serious institutions present serious information to senior readers.

## 3. HOW REFERENCES ARE USED

Use of references is operational, not decorative.

For any LENS visual contract:

1. **Pre-design** — declare which reference families anchor the contract's intent.
2. **In-design** — keep the relevant reference deep-dives open during composition.
3. **Inspection** — evaluate the result against each reference family's borrow / avoid pair.
4. **Validation** — fail the design if it violates an *avoid* clause regardless of how well it satisfies a *borrow* clause.

The avoid clauses are stronger than the borrow clauses. Borrowing wrongly is a quality issue. Violating an avoid is a positioning issue, which is worse.

---

## 4. THE LENS REGION INSIDE THE BOUNDING SET

LENS is not located at any single reference. LENS is located in the *intersection*:

- the operational gravity of Palantir
- the density discipline of Bloomberg
- the spatial restraint of visionOS
- the calm of Linear
- the directness of Raycast
- the atmosphere of Arc
- the presentation quality of Stripe / Vercel
- the consequence weight of NASA / SpaceX
- the editorial hierarchy of FT / institutional briefings

A surface that hits one but violates another is mis-positioned. The doctrine demands the *intersection*, not any single signature.

---

## 5. EXPLICIT NON-REFERENCES

LENS visual contracts must explicitly **not** reference:

- ChatGPT, Claude, or other AI assistant UIs.
- Datadog, Grafana, New Relic, or other observability dashboards.
- Mixpanel, Amplitude, Heap, or other analytics dashboards.
- Notion, ClickUp, Asana, or other productivity SaaS.
- Crypto/web3 dashboards.
- Gaming HUDs.
- Cyberpunk / synthwave aesthetics.

Inspiration drawn from any of these is a doctrine violation regardless of execution quality.
