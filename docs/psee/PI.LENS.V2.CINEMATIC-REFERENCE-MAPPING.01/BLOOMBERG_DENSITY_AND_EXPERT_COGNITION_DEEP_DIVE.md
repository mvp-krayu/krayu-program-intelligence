## 0. WHY THIS REFERENCE

Bloomberg Terminal is the operational reference for *expert-grade information surfaces*. It is the proof that:

- density and seriousness can coexist
- experienced operators reward information hierarchy over decoration
- ugly is acceptable when truthful — but is not aspirational
- trust is built by consistency over four decades, not by polish

LENS borrows the *cognition contract* — high-trust, high-information-density, expert-calibrated. LENS does **not** borrow the *visual signature* (Bloomberg's terminal aesthetic is a legacy artifact, not a target).

---

## 1. CORE QUALITIES TO STUDY

### 1.1 Confidence through clarity

The Bloomberg surface communicates confidence through structural clarity, not through visual flourish. There is no "wow" moment — only an "I always know where to look" moment. That is the cognition LENS is calibrated against.

### 1.2 Serious operational energy

The surface treats the reader as a professional operator who is paid to read the surface. There is no onboarding, no hand-holding, no "tour." The surface assumes competence and rewards it.

### 1.3 Information hierarchy discipline

Every screen has a fixed, learnable hierarchy. The reader knows, after a short period of use, exactly where any given category of information will appear. The discipline is severe and consistent.

### 1.4 Trust earned by reproducibility

The same query produces the same surface. The surface does not change shape across sessions. The reader can trust their muscle memory.

---

## 2. WHAT LENS BORROWS

- **Information hierarchy as primary navigation.** The reader navigates LENS by reading, not by clicking, exactly as a Bloomberg user navigates by glance.
- **Reproducibility of layout.** A LENS state with the same upstream truth produces the same surface composition every time.
- **Density without ornament.** LENS may reach densities that a marketing-grade SaaS would consider excessive, provided every dense element is operationally justified.
- **Expert-calibrated language.** LENS does not over-explain. It assumes the reader knows what "structural readiness" means.

---

## 3. WHAT LENS MUST AVOID

- **Legacy terminal ugliness.** Bloomberg's color palette and typography are constrained by 40-year compatibility, not by design intent. LENS is not.
- **Overwhelming textual compression.** Bloomberg packs three lines of text into the visual space LENS gives one. LENS is built for executives, not for traders.
- **Inaccessible expert overload.** Bloomberg requires hundreds of hours of training to use. LENS must be readable by a senior operator who has never seen it before.
- **Function-key dependence.** Bloomberg's interaction model is a keyboard-shortcut grammar. LENS does not rely on memorized commands on the executive surface.

---

## 4. TYPOGRAPHY READ

| Quality            | Bloomberg                  | LENS direction                                |
|--------------------|----------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Family             | Compressed monospace + sans| Editorial humanist sans, no monospace primary |
| Body size          | Very small (8–10px)        | Generous, readable at executive distance      |
| Line-height        | Tight                      | Open                                          |
| Hierarchy          | Color + size + position    | Size + weight + spacing primary; color minimal|
| Contrast           | Hard-coded high            | High, but tonal, never harsh                  |

LENS borrows the principle that **type carries structure**. LENS rejects the *implementation* — Bloomberg's type is calibrated for traders at three monitors, not for executives at one.

---

## 5. COLOR READ

| Quality              | Bloomberg                 | LENS direction                          |
|----------------------|---------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Ground               | Black                     | Graphite (never black)                  |
| Primary text         | Amber / orange / cyan     | Off-white, modulated by tier            |
| State color          | Heavy and saturated       | Restrained, only on state changes       |
| Decorative color     | None                      | None                                    |
| Category color       | Heavy                     | None on executive surface               |

LENS specifically rejects Bloomberg's amber-on-black signature. LENS uses a graphite ground with restrained steel-blue accents.

---

## 6. INFORMATION DENSITY MODEL

Bloomberg shows the upper bound of density that an expert can absorb. LENS sits well below this bound for two reasons:

1. The LENS reader has minutes, not hours.
2. The LENS reader has not been trained to use the surface.

Practical translation:

- Bloomberg-tier density is permitted in *descent* zones for operators who chose descent.
- The *primary* LENS surface stays at executive density: declaration + implication + propagation + evidence anchor + structural cross-section.

---

## 7. INTERACTION MODEL READ

Bloomberg's interaction model is keyboard-driven, command-grammar-based, and assumes ongoing professional use.

LENS's interaction model is read-driven, hover-revealing, single-click descent, and assumes occasional executive use.

LENS does **not** borrow Bloomberg's interaction model.

---

## 8. INSPECTION CUES (FOR LENS REVIEW PASSES)

When inspecting LENS:

1. Is the information hierarchy as disciplined as Bloomberg's?
2. Is the layout reproducible — does the same upstream truth produce the same surface?
3. Has LENS reached Bloomberg-tier density on the primary surface? (FAIL — must be reduced)
4. Has LENS adopted Bloomberg-style type/color signatures? (FAIL — must be removed)
5. Does LENS require trained-operator behavior to read? (FAIL — must be readable cold)

---

## 9. THE BLOOMBERG LESSON LENS MUST INTERNALIZE

Bloomberg's deepest lesson is that **discipline beats decoration over time**.

Bloomberg has never been "beautiful." It has been *correct*. Forty years of correctness compound into a trust that no SaaS competitor has been able to displace.

LENS does not need to be ugly. But LENS does need to choose discipline over decoration every time the choice arises.

---

## 10. SUMMARY

Bloomberg's gift to LENS is two sentences:

1. **"The surface assumes a competent reader and rewards that assumption."**
2. **"Discipline of hierarchy and reproducibility, sustained over time, becomes trust."**

Both sentences are borrowed in full.

The terminal aesthetic, the color signature, the keyboard grammar — none of those are borrowed.
