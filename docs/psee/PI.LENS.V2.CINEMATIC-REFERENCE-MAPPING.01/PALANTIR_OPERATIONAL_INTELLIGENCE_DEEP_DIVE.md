# PALANTIR (Gotham / Foundry) — Operational Intelligence Deep Dive

**Stream:** PI.LENS.V2.CINEMATIC-REFERENCE-MAPPING.01
**Reference family:** Operational Intelligence
**Doctrine binding:** `VISUAL_DIRECTION_DOCTRINE.md` §1, §3, §5.5

---

## 0. WHY THIS REFERENCE

Palantir's operational surfaces (Gotham, Foundry) are the modern reference for institution-grade investigative computing. They are not aesthetically perfect, but they are the closest existing reference to what LENS is *about*: a serious operator looking at structural reality under consequence.

LENS draws from this family the *positioning* and *gravity*. LENS does **not** draw the *visual signature*.

---

## 1. CORE QUALITIES TO STUDY

### 1.1 Operational gravity

Palantir surfaces communicate that what is shown matters. This is achieved through:

- A consistently dark, restrained ground.
- A near-total absence of marketing register.
- A tone that assumes the reader is a serious operator.
- The avoidance of consumer-product UI patterns.

The surface speaks as if the reader is mid-investigation, not browsing.

### 1.2 Investigative seriousness

The surface treats data as evidence. This shows up as:

- Provenance is visible, not hidden.
- Lineage from claim to source is part of the surface, not a separate page.
- Confidence is bounded, not assumed.
- The reader can trace any assertion back to a source.

### 1.3 Evidence-first atmosphere

The surface does not pretend to know more than it does. Empty states, partial states, and uncertainty states are first-class. Nothing is filled in to look complete.

### 1.4 Command-center realism

The surface is dense, but density serves operational reading. Information is co-located by *operational adjacency*, not by visual symmetry. Nothing is on the surface unless an operator would actually look at it during the workflow the surface supports.

---

## 2. WHAT LENS BORROWS

LENS adopts:

- **Operational gravity** — dark graphite ground, restrained register, no marketing tone.
- **Investigative seriousness** — visible evidence anchoring, confidence bounded by evidence.
- **Evidence-first atmosphere** — honest empty/partial states, no synthetic filling.
- **Operational adjacency** — co-locate elements that an executive actually reads together.

These borrows directly satisfy `VISUAL_DIRECTION_DOCTRINE.md` §3 (Visual Positioning) and §5.5 (Structural Seriousness).

---

## 3. WHAT LENS MUST AVOID

LENS rejects:

- **Military intimidation aesthetic.** Palantir surfaces sometimes lean into a tactical / operations-center feel that reads as defense-industry. LENS is institutional / executive, not tactical / military.
- **Cluttered intelligence overload.** Palantir surfaces tolerate density that a senior executive will not. The LENS reader has 90 seconds, not 30 minutes.
- **Tactical visual aggression.** Heavy use of orange / amber alerting, bracket framing, and grid-warning visual language belongs to investigative analysts, not to executive decision-makers.
- **Tooling chrome.** Palantir surfaces frequently expose tool affordances (filters, queries, transforms) on the primary view. LENS hides all tooling on the executive surface.

---

## 4. TYPOGRAPHY READ

Palantir's typography is functional, not editorial. LENS borrows the *seriousness* but pushes further toward *editorial pacing* (see `INSTITUTIONAL_BRIEFING_TYPOGRAPHY_DEEP_DIVE.md`).

| Quality                | Palantir          | LENS direction                                |
|------------------------|-------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Family register         | Functional sans   | Editorial sans, more humanistic               |
| Declaration weight      | Medium            | Heavy, with deliberate tracking               |
| Body register           | Compressed        | Open, line-height ≥ 1.45                      |
| Label register          | All-caps, dense   | Selective uppercase, generous letter-spacing  |
| Reading register        | Investigator      | Executive briefing                            |

---

## 5. COLOR READ

| Quality              | Palantir                              | LENS direction                          |
|----------------------|---------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Ground               | Near-black to deep slate              | Graphite, never pure black              |
| Primary accent       | Tactical amber / orange               | Restrained steel blue                   |
| State color usage    | Heavy, includes amber alert states    | Restrained, only on real state changes  |
| Saturation           | Moderate                              | Lower, eye-comfortable                  |
| Decorative color     | Some (category coding)                | None                                    |

LENS deliberately steps away from the tactical orange/amber palette. Steel blue with restrained warm accents reads as institutional rather than tactical.

---

## 6. COMPOSITION READ

Palantir surfaces are typically:

- multi-pane
- co-located by workflow
- denser than executive surfaces should be

LENS preserves the *workflow co-location* idea (declaration adjacent to implication adjacent to evidence) but rejects the *multi-pane density*. LENS uses a single dominant declaration zone with descent paths, not parallel investigative panes.

---

## 7. MOTION READ

Palantir's surfaces are largely static. Motion is rare and when present is functional (panel resize, data refresh).

LENS borrows the **restraint**. LENS adds *atmospheric* drift and friction-heavy easing for hover transitions, going beyond Palantir's largely-static surfaces. The result is calmer than Palantir but not static.

---

## 8. INSPECTION CUES (FOR LENS REVIEW PASSES)

When inspecting a LENS surface, ask:

1. Does the surface read with operational gravity comparable to a Palantir investigation surface?
2. Is evidence visibly anchored, or has the LENS surface drifted into "trust me" claims?
3. Has LENS regressed into Palantir's tactical / military register? (FAIL)
4. Has LENS regressed into Palantir's tooling-chrome pattern? (FAIL)
5. Is LENS denser than the executive cognitive budget? (FAIL — LENS must be less dense than Palantir)

---

## 9. WHAT LENS IS *NOT* TRYING TO LOOK LIKE

Palantir is a useful gravity reference but a poor visual template. LENS surfaces should never:

- Be screenshotted next to Palantir and look like a clone.
- Lean into bracketed corner framing, tactical reticle motifs, or grid-warning chrome.
- Use amber/orange as the primary state color.
- Display tooling affordances on the executive surface.

If a LENS reviewer says "this looks like Gotham," that is *not* a compliment. The correct compliment is "this has operational gravity comparable to Gotham, but it reads as institutional, not tactical."

---

## 10. SUMMARY

Palantir's gift to LENS is a single sentence: **"The surface treats the reader as a serious operator working under consequence."**

That sentence is borrowed in full.

Palantir's visual signature, however, is left where it is.
