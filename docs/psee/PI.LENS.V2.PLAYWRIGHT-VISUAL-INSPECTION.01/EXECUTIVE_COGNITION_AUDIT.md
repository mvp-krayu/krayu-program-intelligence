## 1. Reader profile alignment (model §1)

The model assumes a senior operator, < 10 minutes to form belief, no engineer / analyst behavior expected.

- **Before:** The monospace typography and 5-block status sidebar pushed the reader role toward analyst / power-user. The exposed stream code reinforced "internal tool" reading. **MISALIGNED**.
- **After:** Humanist sans, dominant declaration, calm anchor, atmospheric ground, operational identity. The surface now reads as "this is for me, the operator" rather than "this is internal tooling I'm peeking at." **ALIGNED**.

## 3. What executives do not optimize for (model §3)

The surface must NOT assume reader will inspect graph nodes individually, click through filters, compose queries, interpret raw metrics, traverse hierarchies recreationally.

- **Before:** Density toggles + boardroom toggle exposed prominently; 5 status tiles invited "look at all the numbers" reading; evidence grid invited tile-by-tile click pattern. Several violations.
- **After:** Density toggles demoted; status anchor demoted; evidence grid composes a dominant block + supporting context, not a tile-array. Closer to alignment, with one residual: the density toggles still exist on the primary surface and could be moved fully off-surface in a future iteration.
