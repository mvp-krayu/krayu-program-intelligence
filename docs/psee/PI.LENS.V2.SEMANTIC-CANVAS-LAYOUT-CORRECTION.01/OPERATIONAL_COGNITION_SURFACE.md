# OPERATIONAL COGNITION SURFACE

**Stream:** PI.LENS.V2.SEMANTIC-CANVAS-LAYOUT-CORRECTION.01

---

## 1. The thesis

LENS V2 is **not a report viewer**. LENS V2 is **not a dashboard**. LENS V2 is an **operational cognition surface** — a surface designed for an executive operator to *think with*, not just look at.

Before this stream, the LENS V2 surface behaved more like a side-panel dashboard: a wall of executive prose on the left, a cramped semantic rail on the right, and atmospheric ground filling the middle. The middle was visually unused; the right was overloaded; the left was static and dominant.

This stream restructured the surface so that the **center** becomes the **operational cognition field** — the part the executive *uses* — and the left and right become its supporting interpretation and support layers.

---

## 2. What "operational cognition" means here

A cognition surface is a surface where the eye *does work* on a problem, not just receives information. Concretely:

- The dominant region must invite *interrogation*, not just *reading*.
- Semantic objects must be visible, weighted, and spatially distinct.
- Pressure / flow / propagation must read as *action*, not as static text.
- Mode shifts must materially change the cognition — different lens, different tools.

The center canvas now realizes these properties:

- **BALANCED** — the consequence path is visible as horizontal flow with tier-graded anchors.
- **DENSE** — the structural topology is spatial with Coordination Layer as the dominant absorbing actor.
- **INVESTIGATION** — the trace bands carry per-band tier rails and confidence rows.
- **BOARDROOM** — the atmospheric mark embodies the operational posture as projected gravity.

Each mode is recognizably *different*, not a tile-rearrangement of the same content.

---

## 3. Composition principles applied

### 3.1 The center is the cognition surface

The previous layout treated the center as void/atmosphere. This iteration treats the center as the primary surface and the void elsewhere becomes purposeful.

**Operational consequence:** the executive's eye lands on the center after the declaration. The center carries the dominant share of horizontal real estate (~56%).

### 3.2 The left interprets, never dominates

Executive narrative is a *companion* to operational cognition, not the main act. A wall of prose makes the surface read as an executive briefing artifact (which the static reports already are). LENS V2 is what happens *next*.

**Operational consequence:** narrative typography compressed (13/12/11px ladder), left column reduced to ~280px, the narrative supports the canvas rather than competing.

### 3.3 The right supports, never overloads

Evidence state, qualifier state, and Report Pack access belong on the surface — they need to be visible — but they should never compete with the cognition zone.

**Operational consequence:** all support content lives in a single compact right rail; the previous horizontal Report Pack band is removed.

### 3.4 Mode shifts must be material

If a lens change does not visibly *change what the surface is doing*, the lens metaphor is decorative. The four lenses now exhibit clear material differentiation:

- BALANCED: horizontal anchors → CEO consequence path.
- DENSE: spatial topology → CTO structural cause and propagation.
- INVESTIGATION: vertical trace bands → Analyst evidence trace and confidence.
- BOARDROOM: atmospheric mark → projection-grade institutional gravity.

**Operational consequence:** four operational tools, not one tool with four chrome variants.

### 3.5 Semantic zones are visual actors

Pressure tier, propagation role, grounding state, qualifier — these are no longer chips at the corner of a panel. They are encoded as glow halos, weighted nodes, tier rails, and atmospheric color shifts inside the center canvas.

**Operational consequence:** the executive reads pressure tier *spatially*, not by reading a tag.

## 4. The reader contract

The corrected surface assumes:

- A senior operator with under 90 seconds for a directional read.
- A single dominant focal element after the declaration: the center canvas.
- Persona lenses that change the surface, not the chrome.
- Honest uncertainty — qualifier state is always visible somewhere.
- No demand to *click around* before the surface communicates anything.

The reader does not have to discover what LENS V2 is doing. The reader sees it.

---

## 5. What "not a dashboard" means concretely

The center canvas, by design, does NOT contain:

- KPI tile grids.
- Bar / line / sparkline charts.
- Donut / pie / dial visualizations.
- Filter rows or time-range pickers.
- "+ Add widget" affordances.
- Equal-weight card matrices.
- Any element that would let the executive say "this is just another analytics tool."

Instead it contains: a small set of operational semantic actors composed deliberately to communicate one structural truth at a time.

---

## 6. What "not a report viewer" means concretely

LENS V2 does NOT inline static report HTML. The static Tier-1 / Tier-2 reports remain commercially valid sellable artifacts and are accessed through the Report Pack in the support rail — not embedded in the cognition surface. The executive can hand-off to the static reports for deep evidence; LENS V2 is for the directional read.

## 7. Operational gravity — the visual signature

A successful cognition surface produces a sensation of *operational gravity* — the sense that what is shown matters and that the system is taking the situation seriously. The corrected surface achieves this through:

- The compressed authority band ("Program · Delivery Coordination").
- The dominant 64px declaration with negative tracking.
- The qualifier mandate band when active.
- The center canvas with mode-weighted semantic actors.
- The atmospheric ground gradients.
- The calm support rail with honest binding-state markers.

This is the visual signature of a tool built for consequence.
