## Purpose

This document defines the executive narrative transformation strategy and anti-jargon doctrine applied in EXPERIENCE-REFINEMENT.01.

---

## I. The Problem: Ontology-Driven Language

The prior narrative language was generated by adapters that extracted raw field values from governance structures. The result was accurate but unrelatable:

**Example (prior):**
> "Primary Delivery domain is operating under high execution pressure. Pressure has propagated into Coordination Layer with elevated intensity."

This language:
- Describes internal system ontology, not organizational reality
- Uses passive structural framing ("has propagated into")
- Has no executive consequence visibility
- Sounds like a log entry, not intelligence

---

## II. The Transformation Strategy

### Principle 1: Name Organizational Reality

Move from describing internal system nodes to describing organizational behavior.

| Before | After |
|---|---|
| "Primary Delivery domain" | "Critical delivery operations" |
| "Coordination Layer" | "the program coordination layer" |
| "Secondary Delivery" | "Secondary delivery pipelines" |

The words "domain" and "layer" are system vocabulary. Executives understand "delivery operations," "program coordination," and "delivery pipelines."

### Principle 2: Describe What Is Happening, Not What Was Detected

Move from signal detection language to operational consequence language.

| Before | After |
|---|---|
| "pressure has propagated" | "execution instability has migrated" |
| "absorbed 68% of propagated pressure load" | "has absorbed 68% of propagated load — a pattern consistent with organizational stress migration" |
| "Pressure detected" | "Moderate pressure detected" → "structural exposure may exceed what is currently measurable" |

Detection language describes the sensor. Consequence language describes the organization.

### Principle 3: Make Advisory Constraints Explicit and Human

The Q-01 qualifier cannot be softened. But it can be explained in executive terms.

| Before | After |
|---|---|
| "full pressure extent may be underrepresented in this view" | "the full operational extent may be underrepresented due to incomplete grounding" |
| "Advisory confirmation recommended" | "Executive action on that signal requires advisory confirmation before commitment" |

The executive must understand: what are they being asked to do? Confirm before committing. That is now explicit.

### Principle 4: Distinguish Generating vs. Conducting Pressure

A key organizational insight in this report is that the Coordination Layer is not generating stress — it is conducting it. This distinction matters to executives making intervention decisions.

| Before | After |
|---|---|
| "Coordination Layer has absorbed propagated pressure from Primary Delivery" | "Program coordination is conducting instability originating from Primary Delivery — not generating independent load" |

An executive who understands this will focus intervention on the origin, not the conductor.

## III. Anti-Jargon Doctrine

The following language patterns are FORBIDDEN in executive-facing narrative:

| Pattern | Problem | Alternative |
|---|---|---|
| "domain X is operating under pressure" | Ontology-speak | "X operations are under load" / "X is under sustained pressure" |
| "pressure has propagated into Y" | Passive machine-semantics | "instability has migrated into Y" / "Y is absorbing pressure from X" |
| "cluster-level pressure concentration" | Unmapped technical concept | "23 of 31 delivery clusters are above threshold" |
| "evidence cycles" | Internal measurement language | "evidence cycles" → acceptable if explained as "consecutive measurement periods" |
| "propagation role: ORIGIN" | Raw ontology | "originates in X" |
| "PASS_THROUGH" | System enum | "conducting pressure" / "absorbing and transmitting" |
| "RECEIVER" | System enum | "receiving downstream pressure" / "absorbing impact" |

## IV. Readability Doctrine

### 4.1 Section Labels

Section labels are navigational — they tell the executive what they are about to read.

**Requirement:** Section labels must be visible. They must use operational vocabulary.

| Before | After |
|---|---|
| `READINESS ASSESSMENT` | `OPERATIONAL POSTURE` |
| `EVIDENCE DEPTH` | `SIGNAL EVIDENCE` |
| `STRUCTURAL TOPOLOGY` | `PROPAGATION STRUCTURE` |
| `WHY THIS MATTERS` (retained) | `WHY THIS MATTERS` |
| `EXECUTIVE INTELLIGENCE` | `EXECUTIVE ASSESSMENT` |

### 4.2 Status Panel

The status panel (right column) shows summary-level state signals. Labels must be executive-relatable.

| Before | After |
|---|---|
| `MOTION PROFILE` | Removed (developer concept) |
| `URGENCY` | Removed (not directly actionable here) |
| `STATE` | `READINESS` |
| `QUALIFIER` | `QUALIFIER` (retained) |
| (absent) | `DOMAINS` — executive scope indicator |
| (absent) | `COVERAGE` — grounding confidence indicator |
| (absent) | `CLUSTERS` — evidence depth indicator |

## V. Cognition Flow Doctrine

The executive reading sequence must be visible in the interface:

1. **OPERATIONAL POSTURE** — What is the verdict?
   - Large state declaration, state-reactive color, left anchor

2. **QUALIFIER MANDATE** — Is there a constraint I must acknowledge?
   - Full-width amber band, non-suppressable, mandatory-grade language

3. **EXECUTIVE ASSESSMENT** — What does this mean for the organization?
   - High contrast prose, 16px, left accent line
   - Followed by WHY and STRUCTURAL CONTEXT

4. **PROPAGATION STRUCTURE** — How did this happen? Which parts of the organization are involved?
   - Horizontal topology chain, role and pressure visible per domain

5. **SIGNAL EVIDENCE** — What is the underlying signal supporting this?
   - Evidence blocks per domain, grounding status visible

This sequence is enforced by visual hierarchy, not just layout order.

---

## VI. Narrative Governance

Executive language refinement must not violate evidence governance:

- **MUST NOT speculate** beyond available evidence
- **MUST NOT soften** qualifier implications
- **MUST NOT imply certainty** where grounding is partial
- **MUST NOT rewrite** evidence into conclusions
- **MUST preserve** all advisory language

The Q-01 qualifier language ("advisory confirmation required", "the full operational extent may be underrepresented") is mandatory and must appear in its strongest form, not its softest.

This is not a matter of style — it is a governance constraint.
