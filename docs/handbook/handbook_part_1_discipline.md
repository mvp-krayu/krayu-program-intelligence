PROGRAM INTELLIGENCE HANDBOOK
Part I — Discipline

Source Stream:
10 — Program Intelligence Discipline

Purpose

This part introduces the doctrinal foundations of Program Intelligence.

The discipline defines why Program Intelligence exists, the problems it addresses, and the principles that guide its operation.

Program Intelligence emerged from the recognition that traditional program reporting fails to reveal the real condition of program execution. Status reports often describe activity but fail to expose structural delivery conditions.

The discipline therefore focuses on observable execution behavior.

Core principles of the discipline include:

• Evidence-first analysis
• Observable execution behavior
• Signal-based interpretation
• Structural understanding of delivery systems

Artifacts in Stream 10 define:

• the origin of the discipline
• the doctrinal principles
• the terminology of Program Intelligence
• the intellectual intent of the discipline

This part of the handbook introduces those foundations and explains their role in the broader Program Intelligence system.
