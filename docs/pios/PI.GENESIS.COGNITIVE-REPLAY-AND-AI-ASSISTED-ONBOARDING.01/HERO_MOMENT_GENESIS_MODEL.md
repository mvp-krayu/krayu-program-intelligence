# HERO MOMENT GENESIS MODEL

> **Status:** LOCKED — Canonical model for Hero Moment emergence in cognitive genesis.

---

## 1. Definition

A **Hero Moment** is a governed cognitive discontinuity — a point where the system discovers a non-obvious, evidence-backed insight during any phase of cognition formation.

Hero Moments are not downstream semantic artifacts. They are not manufactured for narrative impact. They are not exclusive to S2 or semantic qualification.

**Hero Moments can appear at S1 / PATH A state.**

They emerge whenever the system encounters non-obvious governed reality.

---

Hero Moments may emerge at any phase of cognitive genesis:

## 4. Hero Moment Detection Contract

A valid Hero Moment must satisfy ALL of the following:

1. **Evidence-backed:** Grounded in structural or semantic evidence, not narrative inference
2. **Non-obvious:** Would surprise a knowledgeable observer of the client's architecture
3. **Governed:** Captured through governed pipeline phases, not manual inspection
4. **Replayable:** Would be discovered again on deterministic replay of the same inputs
5. **Unmanufactured:** NOT invented for narrative impact, commercial value, or demonstration

### Detection Triggers

```
STRUCTURAL:
  inbound_degree > threshold AND unexpected_for_domain  → Authority Surprise
  cross_domain_rate > expected                          → Coupling Surprise
  circular_dependency_detected                          → Coupling Surprise
  node_classification != expected_role                  → Architecture Surprise

SEMANTIC:
  grounding_conflict(evidence, claim)                   → Grounding Surprise
  domain_count != expected                              → Semantic Surprise
  proposition_reveals_hidden_relationship               → Structural Claim Surprise

GOVERNANCE:
  operator_rejection_reveals_pipeline_defect             → Friction Surprise
  enrichment_fundamentally_changes_understanding        → Enrichment Surprise
  replay_divergence_from_forward_pass                   → Replay Surprise
```

## 6. Hero Moment Lifecycle

```
CANDIDATE
  → discovered by pipeline or AI-assisted analysis
  → evidence refs populated
  → structural metric captured

REVIEWED
  → operator examines evidence
  → confirms non-obviousness
  → may add annotation

CONFIRMED
  → operator accepts as genuine Hero Moment
  → eligible for chronicle inclusion
  → learning event may be emitted

REJECTED
  → operator determines it is expected, trivial, or fabricated
  → preserved in lineage (rejection is governance, not deletion)
```

## 7. Hero Moments in the Chronicle

When the chronicle captures cognitive genesis (not just semantic replay), Hero Moments become structural chapter markers:

- A Hero Moment at Phase 3 (topology) marks a FORMATION-phase cognitive discontinuity
- A Hero Moment at Phase 3.7 (centrality) marks a STRENGTHENING-phase structural revelation
- A Hero Moment at governance review marks a TENSION-phase friction discovery

The chronicle does not need Hero Moments at every phase. But when they exist, they are the points where the narrative gains structural weight — the moments when the system reveals something the audience did not expect.

## 8. Cross-Specimen Hero Moment Convergence

When multiple specimens produce Hero Moments of the same type:

- 2 specimens: **OBSERVED** — comparison only, DESCRIPTIVE maturity
- 3+ specimens: eligible for **PATTERN** promotion — may indicate a reusable detection heuristic

Hero Moment convergence follows the same maturity discipline as convergence observations: observational, not unification. Different clients may produce structurally different hero moments. The value is in the diversity of governed cognitive discontinuities, not in forcing specimens into the same surprise.

## 9. What Hero Moments Are NOT

- NOT manufactured for commercial demonstration
- NOT exclusive to semantic qualification (S2+)
- NOT exclusive to PATH B
- NOT AI-generated narrative flourishes
- NOT required for every onboarding (some clients have boring topology — that is valid)
- NOT a metric of onboarding quality (absence of surprise is not failure)
