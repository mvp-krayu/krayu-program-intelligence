# Proto-Dynamic CEU Classification

**Stream:** PI.SQO.DYNAMIC-CEU.RE-GOVERNANCE-AND-CLASSIFICATION.01.REVISED
**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Status:** SPECIFICATION — no runtime implementation
**Dependency:** HISTORICAL_AUTHORITY_LOAD_REPORT.md (complete)

---

## 1. Purpose

This document classifies all proto-Dynamic-CEU concepts — the pre-formalization
antecedents that existed before the current-era canonical Dynamic CEU architecture.
Each concept is assigned a classification:

- **A (CANONICAL)** — compatible with current governance, preserved intact
- **B (RE-GOVERNABLE)** — needs reformulation but the core idea survives
- **C (HISTORICALLY IMPORTANT BUT PROHIBITED)** — shaped governance evolution but is now explicitly prohibited
- **D (OBSOLETE)** — no longer relevant to the architecture

---

## 2. Early Era Proto-Concepts

### P-01: PSIG Signal Enrichment

**Source:** SEMANTIC_GOVERNANCE_EXPLORATION_CLOSURE.md (21-stream family)
**Era:** Early
**Description:** Enrich PSIG signals in the 75.x activation layer with semantic
context derived from structural analysis.

**Classification: D (OBSOLETE)**

**Rationale:** The enrichment target shifted from signals to qualification state.
DPSIG is Lane D sovereign and immutable. Signal enrichment as a Dynamic CEU
direction was explicitly abandoned when the governance exploration concluded.
The concept has no forward path in the current architecture.

---

### P-02: Semantic Activation Authority

**Source:** SEMANTIC_GOVERNANCE_EXPLORATION_CLOSURE.md
**Era:** Early
**Description:** The authority to declare semantic truth — to assign semantic
meaning to structural artifacts with authoritative force.

**Classification: C (HISTORICALLY IMPORTANT BUT PROHIBITED)**

**Rationale:** This concept was explored across 21 streams and INTENTIONALLY BLOCKED.
It is historically important because the governance exploration shaped the entire
boundary model. It is prohibited because Dynamic CEU activates EVIDENCE for
evaluation, not semantic authority for declaration. The distinction between evidence
authority and semantic authority is foundational to the entire current architecture.

**Governance enforcement:** Pre-activation requirement: "Source authority is external."
Immutability boundary: cannot override Q-class formula or bypass S-state gates.

---

### P-03: Structural-Relational Intelligence

**Source:** SEMANTIC_GOVERNANCE_EXPLORATION_CLOSURE.md
**Era:** Early
**Description:** The ability to observe and characterize structural relationships
without claiming semantic authority over their meaning.

**Classification: A (CANONICAL)**

**Rationale:** This concept survives intact. The distinction between observation
(structural-relational intelligence) and activation (semantic authority) is the
foundational boundary of Dynamic CEU. Structural-relational intelligence is what
the deterministic pipeline produces. Dynamic CEU operates in the space between
structural observation and semantic authority — adding governed evidence that
augments evaluation.

---

### P-04: CEU Grounding Classification (GROUNDED/NON_GROUNDED/DERIVED/INVALID)

**Source:** GROUNDING_DESIGN_SPECIFICATION.md
**Era:** Early
**Description:** Classify CEU mappings as GROUNDED (pipeline-verified),
NON_GROUNDED (no pipeline evidence), DERIVED (computed from grounded sources),
or INVALID (contaminated).

**Classification: A (CANONICAL)**

**Rationale:** This classification system is the basis of Static CEU. It survives
intact as the certified substrate that Dynamic CEU augments but never modifies.
The 4-tier classification is the immutable truth that overlays cannot touch.

---

### P-05: Contamination Prevention Model

**Source:** GROUNDING_DESIGN_SPECIFICATION.md
**Era:** Early
**Description:** Prevent contamination of grounded mappings by forbidden sources.
9 forbidden mapping sources (FM-1 through FM-9). Strict isolation rules.

**Classification: B (RE-GOVERNABLE)**

**Rationale:** The core concern — preventing contamination of certified truth by
unauthorized sources — survives and is critical. But the specific implementation
(forbidden source lists FM-1 through FM-9) has been RE-GOVERNED as the immutability
boundary model. The 9 protected elements in the Static/Dynamic CEU separation
are the current-era formalization of contamination prevention. The forbidden source
model is subsumed, not abandoned.

---

### P-06: Allowed Mapping Sources (MS-1 through MS-6)

**Source:** GROUNDING_DESIGN_SPECIFICATION.md
**Era:** Early
**Description:** 6 allowed mapping source types that could produce grounded
CEU classifications.

**Classification: B (RE-GOVERNABLE)**

**Rationale:** The concept of authorized sources for evidence survives as the
semantic class authorization model (7 classes, source-to-class authorization
mapping). The specific MS-1 through MS-6 list is subsumed by the more
comprehensive class-based authorization model. The core idea (not all sources
are equally authoritative) is preserved.

---

### P-07: 11-Step Mapping Algorithm

**Source:** GROUNDING_DESIGN_SPECIFICATION.md
**Era:** Early
**Description:** Deterministic 11-step algorithm for grounding CEU mappings.

**Classification: A (CANONICAL) — for Static CEU only**

**Rationale:** The 11-step algorithm applies to Static CEU grounding — the
pipeline-certified mapping process. It does NOT apply to Dynamic CEU, which
uses a different process (SEP creation → staging → activation → re-evaluation).
The algorithm is canonical for its domain but does not extend to Dynamic CEU.

## 3. Mid Era Proto-Concepts

### P-08: CEU Paradox

**Source:** DYNAMIC_CEU_GOVERNANCE_MODEL.md
**Era:** Mid
**Description:** High structural CEU grounding does NOT imply semantic maturity.
FastAPI: 0.90 structural grounding, 0/9 semantic domains grounded.

**Classification: A (CANONICAL)**

**Rationale:** The CEU Paradox is the foundational motivation for Dynamic CEU.
It explains WHY Dynamic CEU exists: structural completeness and semantic
completeness are different dimensions that require different evidence sources.
The paradox is preserved as the justification for the Static/Dynamic separation.

---

### P-09: CEU as Governed Maturation Assistance

**Source:** DYNAMIC_CEU_GOVERNANCE_MODEL.md
**Era:** Mid
**Description:** Reframe CEU from "Computable Enhancement Unit" to "Governed
Maturation Assistance" — emphasizing that Dynamic CEU helps clients mature
from lower S-states to higher ones.

**Classification: B (RE-GOVERNABLE)**

**Rationale:** The maturation assistance framing is partially preserved in the
qualification re-evaluation model — Dynamic CEU evidence CAN contribute to
S-state advancement. But the current-era framing is more precise: Dynamic CEU
is "evidence-activated additive semantic overlay" rather than "maturation
assistance." The concept is re-governed with more precise terminology.

---

### P-10: 8 Allowable Behaviors

**Source:** DYNAMIC_CEU_GOVERNANCE_MODEL.md
**Era:** Mid
**Description:** 8 behaviors Dynamic CEU is allowed to perform: add labels,
upgrade lineage, extend crosswalk, bind capabilities, enrich edges, assign
domains, provide guidance, gate readiness.

**Classification: B (RE-GOVERNABLE)**

**Rationale:** 6 of 8 behaviors map directly to current-era claim types
(LABEL_ASSIGNMENT, LINEAGE_UPGRADE, CONTINUITY_MAPPING, CAPABILITY_BINDING,
EDGE_ENRICHMENT, DOMAIN_TYPING). The remaining 2 (guidance, readiness gating)
are not Dynamic CEU functions — they are cockpit/evaluation functions. The
behavior list is re-governed as a claim type taxonomy.

---

### P-11: 12 Prohibited Behaviors

**Source:** DYNAMIC_CEU_GOVERNANCE_MODEL.md
**Era:** Mid
**Description:** 12 behaviors Dynamic CEU must never perform, including
substrate mutation, Q-class override, S-state bypass, and others.

**Classification: A (CANONICAL)**

**Rationale:** All 12 prohibited behaviors are enforced in the current era
with strictly more comprehensive governance mechanisms. The prohibition
list survives intact — the enforcement mechanisms have been strengthened.

---

### P-12: 6-Step Enrichment Workflow

**Source:** DYNAMIC_CEU_GOVERNANCE_MODEL.md
**Era:** Mid
**Description:** Propose → Validate → Authorize → Apply → Verify → Record.

**Classification: B (RE-GOVERNABLE)**

**Rationale:** The workflow concept survives but is re-governed as:
- SEP creation (Propose)
- Pre-activation requirements (Validate + Authorize)
- Activation process (Apply)
- Replay verification (Verify)
- Activation event logging (Record)

The 6-step workflow is subsumed by the more detailed activation process.

---

### P-13: Replay-Safe Enrichment Guarantees (R1–R5)

**Source:** REPLAY_SAFE_ENRICHMENT_MODEL.md
**Era:** Mid
**Description:** R1 deterministic derivation, R2 additive-only persistence,
R3 source immutability, R4 hash-anchored provenance, R5 operation versioning.

**Classification: A (CANONICAL)**

**Rationale:** All 5 guarantees are preserved in the SEP architecture.
R1 → deterministic overlay application order.
R2 → overlay isolation (never modifies substrate).
R3 → source hash in provenance chain.
R4 → package-level hashing (extended).
R5 → monotonic integer versioning.

---

### P-14: Overlay Rules (OV-01 through OV-06)

**Source:** REPLAY_SAFE_ENRICHMENT_MODEL.md
**Era:** Mid
**Description:** 6 overlay governance rules for enrichment operations.

**Classification: B (RE-GOVERNABLE)**

**Rationale:** The overlay rules are subsumed by the comprehensive replay-safe
overlay architecture (conflict resolution, ordered application, package hashing,
independent removability). The core governance intent is preserved; the specific
rules are reformulated at higher fidelity.

---

### P-15: Lane Architecture (A/B/C/D)

**Source:** SQO_LANE_ARCHITECTURE.md
**Era:** Mid
**Description:** Lane A (structural, frozen), Lane B (reference), Lane C
(experimental), Lane D (governed migration target / DPSIG sovereign).

**Classification: A (CANONICAL)**

**Rationale:** Lane architecture is preserved as the structural boundary model.
Dynamic CEU operates within the SQO lane (not A, not D). Lane A immutability
and Lane D sovereignty are enforced by the immutability boundary.

---

### P-16: SQO Boundary Contract (MAY/MUST NEVER)

**Source:** SQO_LANE_ARCHITECTURE.md
**Era:** Mid
**Description:** SQO MAY enrich, annotate, qualify, contextualize, recommend,
gate, disclose, stabilize. SQO MUST NEVER mutate Lane A, modify replay
artifacts, alter topology, invent semantics, override Q-class.

**Classification: A (CANONICAL)**

**Rationale:** The SQO boundary contract is preserved intact. The Dynamic CEU
activation boundaries are a formalization of this contract's MUST NEVER list.

---

### P-17: 6-Stage Governed Extension Lifecycle

**Source:** GOVERNED_INTELLIGENCE_EXTENSION_MODEL.md
**Era:** Mid
**Description:** Governed manifest → additive lane → replay-safe integration →
readiness gating → E2E certification → executive cognitive stabilization.

**Classification: A (CANONICAL)**

**Rationale:** The lifecycle model is preserved as the governance process
that Dynamic CEU follows from conceptualization to activation. The SEP
lifecycle (CREATION → STAGED → ACTIVATED → [REVOKED/SUPERSEDED]) maps to
stages 1–4. E2E certification and executive stabilization are downstream
of Dynamic CEU and are preserved as future requirements.
