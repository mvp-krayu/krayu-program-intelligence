## Why This Matters Now

Every commercial artifact so far assumes a direct sales motion:

```
Signäl team → Customer → SA engagement → SC expansion
```

This is correct for launch. But the existing network already includes:

- **Strategic Transformation Advisors** who embed in enterprise change programs
- **Enterprise Advisory Partners** with existing enterprise consulting relationships
- **Technology due diligence specialists** in PE/M&A corridors
- **Architecture advisory practices** running program assessments today

These relationships are not future pipeline. They are current conversations. The question is not "should we have partners?" — it is "what can a partner do with Signäl, and what must Signäl retain?"

---

## Channel Principle

**Partners bring the relationship. Signäl brings the intelligence.**

Partner archetypes are intentionally role-based rather than person-based. Signäl's channel strategy depends on repeatable relationship classes, not individual named relationships. The strategy must remain executable regardless of which specific advisors, firms, or individuals occupy each role at any given time.

This is not a reseller model. Signäl is not a product that a partner sells off a shelf. The structural intelligence pipeline — evidence intake, topology reconstruction, signal synthesis, consequence compilation, Structural Verdict generation — is Signäl's core. No partner operates this pipeline.

What partners bring:

| Partner Capability | Value |
|-------------------|-------|
| Enterprise access | Relationships that would take Signäl 6-18 months to establish directly |
| Transformation context | Understanding of the customer's change program, budget, and decision dynamics |
| Trust | Customer already trusts the partner's judgment. Partner recommending Signäl carries that trust. |
| Advisory wrapper | The partner's broader engagement (transformation program, DD process, architecture review) provides the context in which structural intelligence has maximum impact |
| Deal velocity | Partner identifies the buyer, frames the need, introduces Signäl. First conversation starts at "when can we do this?" not "what is this?" |
| Category amplification | Certain partners accelerate market understanding of Structural Intelligence as a category — through their advisory authority, client conversations, and industry presence |

What Signäl retains:

| Signäl Capability | Why It Cannot Be Delegated |
|-------------------|---------------------------|
| Evidence intake and pipeline execution | Deterministic governance requires Signäl-operated pipeline. Same evidence → same findings. Partner cannot introduce variability. |
| Structural Verdict production | The governed 9-chapter output is produced by Signäl's intelligence pipeline. Partner does not author, edit, or influence findings. |
| LENS provisioning | Platform access is Signäl-operated. Partner may co-deliver the advisory session but does not control what LENS shows. |
| Qualification governance | SQO authority, confidence envelope, governance boundaries — these are Signäl's non-delegable governance substrate. |
| Intelligence pipeline evolution | Pipeline improvements, new Domain Modules, new Cognition Objects — all Signäl-internal. |

**The boundary is clean:** Partners own the relationship and the advisory wrapper. Signäl owns the intelligence and the governance. The Structural Verdict is Signäl's artifact, delivered through or alongside the partner's engagement.

## Category Development Partners

Partners are not only channels. Certain partners contribute something beyond pipeline: **category amplification.**

Structural Intelligence is a new category. The market does not yet have vocabulary for what Signäl reveals. Category understanding must be built — and partners who operate in enterprise advisory, transformation leadership, and due diligence corridors accelerate this process through their existing credibility and client conversations.

| Partner Type | Category Contribution |
|-------------|----------------------|
| **Transformation advisors** | Introduce "structural consequence" language into enterprise change programs. Their clients start asking for structural intelligence because their advisor framed the question. |
| **Strategic consultancies** | Legitimize the category at scale. When a major consulting firm references structural intelligence in a client engagement, the category gains institutional credibility. |
| **Due diligence leaders** | Establish structural assessment as a standard DD component. Once one PE firm receives a Structural Verdict, others expect it. |
| **Industry thought leaders** | Conference presence, published perspectives, advisory board participation. They don't just refer clients — they educate the market. |

**Category Development Partners do not have separate economics or governance.** They participate as Referral, Embedded, or Alliance partners. The distinction is recognition: their value includes market education, not just deal flow. Signäl should invest disproportionately in these relationships — co-presenting, co-publishing, supporting their advisory narrative — because category acceleration compounds across all channels.

## Channel Models

**The commercial center of gravity is the Embedded Partner model.** Referral is tactical — it generates introductions. Embedded is strategic — it generates enterprise engagements where structural intelligence is delivered within the partner's advisory context. Alliance is the mature-state evolution of Embedded after volume and trust are proven.

### Model 1: Referral Partner

**Tactical. Fast to activate. Not the destination.**

```
Partner identifies need → Introduces Signäl → Signäl runs direct engagement → Partner receives referral fee
```

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Partner role** | Identify the buyer, make the introduction, frame the need |
| **Signäl role** | Full direct engagement: scoping, evidence intake, pipeline, Structural Verdict, advisory session, LENS access |
| **Economics** | Referral fee (percentage of SA/SA-DD engagement fee) paid on closed deal |
| **Governance** | Partner does not touch evidence, pipeline, or findings |
| **Who this is for** | Technology advisors with enterprise networks who want to add structural intelligence to their recommended tools. Low commitment. Low complexity. |
| **Activation effort** | Minimal. One conversation. No training required. Partner needs to understand what Signäl reveals, not how it works. |

**When to use:** When the partner's value is access, not delivery. Or as a starting point for a relationship that may evolve to Embedded.

---

### Model 2: Embedded Partner (PRIMARY — STRATEGIC CENTER OF GRAVITY)

**The strategic model. The one that scales.**

```
Partner runs advisory engagement → Signäl provides structural intelligence as embedded component → Deliverables co-branded or Signäl-branded within partner engagement → Partner owns customer relationship
```

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Partner role** | Owns the customer engagement (transformation program, architecture review, DD process). Frames structural intelligence as part of their advisory service. Co-delivers the advisory session alongside Signäl. |
| **Signäl role** | Evidence intake, pipeline execution, Structural Verdict production, LENS provisioning. May co-deliver advisory session with partner. |
| **Economics** | Partner purchases SA/SA-DD at partner rate (discount from published price). Partner sets their own client-facing price within their engagement. Signäl receives the partner rate per assessment. |
| **Governance** | Structural Verdict is Signäl-produced. Partner may present it within their engagement context but may not modify findings, severity classifications, or governance boundaries. Partner may add their own advisory interpretation AROUND the Structural Verdict — not WITHIN it. |
| **Who this is for** | Transformation consultancies, architecture advisory firms, DD specialists. Partners who run multi-week or multi-month engagements where structural intelligence is a deliverable within a larger scope. |
| **Activation effort** | Moderate. Partner needs: (1) Signäl briefing (what it reveals, what it doesn't), (2) Advisory session co-delivery training, (3) Partner agreement on governance boundaries. |

**The key insight:** The partner's transformation engagement or DD process already has a budget, a timeline, and a deliverable set. Signäl becomes one component within that engagement. The partner doesn't need to sell "structural intelligence" as a standalone concept — they sell their transformation/DD engagement and include Signäl as the structural evidence behind their recommendations.

**Example — Transformation advisory:**
1. Partner runs a modernization assessment for an enterprise client
2. Partner engages Signäl for structural intelligence on the target platform
3. Signäl produces Structural Verdict + LENS access
4. Partner uses Signäl's findings to ground their transformation recommendations in structural evidence
5. Client receives the partner's advisory report with the Structural Verdict as a governed appendix or embedded section
6. The advisory session is co-delivered: partner frames the transformation context, Signäl walks through the structural findings

**Example — PE Due Diligence:**
1. PE firm's DD advisor is running technical due diligence on an acquisition target
2. DD advisor engages Signäl for structural assessment (SA-DD)
3. Signäl produces the Structural Verdict + LENS access (all 4 cognitive modes + Investigation Protocol)
4. DD advisor incorporates structural findings into their DD report for the investment committee
5. Signäl's governed Evidence Record provides the audit trail the committee requires

### Model 3: Strategic Alliance

**The enterprise model. Longer-term. Deeper integration.**

```
Alliance partner licenses Signäl capability as part of their service portfolio → Joint go-to-market → Volume commitment
```

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Partner role** | Includes structural intelligence as a standard offering within their consulting practice. Joint marketing. Joint case studies. Dedicated team trained on Signäl co-delivery. |
| **Signäl role** | Dedicated partner support. SLA on evidence intake turnaround. Co-developed advisory playbooks. Volume pricing. |
| **Economics** | Volume commitment at significant partner discount. Annual minimum commitment. Partner sets client pricing. |
| **Governance** | Same as Embedded Partner — Signäl produces all intelligence artifacts. Alliance does not change the governance boundary. |
| **Who this is for** | Major consulting firms, global DD practices, enterprise transformation firms with 50+ engagements per year potential. |
| **Activation effort** | High. Requires: partner agreement, training program, co-delivery playbook, joint marketing material, SLA framework, volume pricing negotiation. |

**When to activate:** Alliance is the maturation of Model 2, not a separate path. Activated when an Embedded Partner demonstrates sustained volume, governance compliance, and co-delivery quality — not after an arbitrary engagement count.

## Partner Economics

### Referral Partner

| Metric | Model |
|--------|-------|
| **Referral fee** | Percentage of closed SA/SA-DD engagement fee (10-20%). Paid on deal close. |
| **SC/SE expansion** | If referred customer converts SA → SC, partner receives referral fee on first year only. No ongoing commission. |
| **Exclusivity** | None. Customer becomes Signäl direct after first engagement. |

### Embedded Partner

| Metric | Model |
|--------|-------|
| **Partner rate** | Discount from published SA/SA-DD price (20-30% below published). |
| **Partner client pricing** | Partner determines. Signäl does not control what the partner charges their client. |
| **Volume incentive** | Additional discount for 5+ assessments per year. Encourages portfolio-wide adoption. |
| **SC/SE conversion** | If partner's client wants SC, Signäl offers SC at direct pricing with assessment credit. Partner may continue as co-delivery partner (with advisory fee). |
| **Revenue share on SC/SE** | Not a revenue share. The partner purchases discrete assessments. SC/SE are direct Signäl relationships — governance authority, platform operation, and runtime responsibility require direct accountability. Partner advisory involvement continues where commercially appropriate. |

### Strategic Alliance

| Metric | Model |
|--------|-------|
| **Volume commitment** | Annual minimum assessment count at significant discount (30-40% below published). |
| **Joint marketing** | Co-branded case studies, joint events, partner listing on Signäl materials. |
| **SC/SE** | Alliance partner may bundle SC into their client engagements at partner rate. SE remains direct Signäl relationship (enterprise infrastructure requires direct support). |
| **Dedicated support** | Named Signäl contact. Accelerated evidence intake SLA. |

## Governance Constraints for All Partner Models

These are non-negotiable. They apply to Referral, Embedded, and Alliance partners equally.

| Constraint | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Partner may not modify the Structural Verdict** | The Verdict is a governed artifact. Deterministic. Same evidence → same findings. Partner modification would break reproducibility and audit lineage. |
| **Partner may not operate the intelligence pipeline** | Evidence intake, signal synthesis, consequence compilation, and topology reconstruction are Signäl-operated. No white-label pipeline. |
| **Partner may not re-brand the Structural Verdict as their own work product** | The Verdict may appear within the partner's engagement context (e.g., as an appendix to a transformation report), but it must be attributed to Signäl. Governed provenance is the product. |
| **Partner may not select or suppress findings** | All findings in the Structural Verdict are structurally grounded. The partner cannot request removal of findings that are inconvenient for their client relationship. |
| **Partner may not override governance boundaries** | Confidence envelope, governance boundary disclosure, and the 13 absolute prohibitions apply regardless of partner context. |
| **Partner may add their own advisory interpretation** | Partners may and should add their advisory perspective. But their interpretation wraps AROUND the Structural Verdict — it does not edit what's INSIDE it. The partner's advisory is their work product. The Structural Verdict is Signäl's governed artifact. Both coexist. |

**Why these constraints protect the partner too:** If a partner modifies findings to please a client and the findings later prove correct, the partner's credibility is destroyed. The unmodifiable Structural Verdict protects the partner from their own client-management instincts. "This is what the structural analysis determined" is the partner's shield, not their constraint.

### Embedded Partner Readiness Criteria

An Embedded Partner may be activated **immediately** — including as the first Signäl engagement — when ALL of the following are satisfied:

| Criterion | Verification |
|-----------|-------------|
| Partner accepts governance boundaries | Written acknowledgment: Structural Verdict is Signäl-produced, unmodifiable, Signäl-attributed |
| Partner accepts Signäl attribution | Signäl provenance preserved in all deliverables containing the Structural Verdict |
| Partner accepts Signäl-operated intelligence pipeline | No white-label, no partner-operated pipeline, no self-service evidence processing |
| Partner has active customer opportunity | A real engagement — not hypothetical pipeline |
| Partner agrees co-delivery model | Joint advisory session format agreed. Evidence intake logistics defined. |

**No prior direct engagement count is required.** The first meaningful SA engagement may arrive through a partner. Readiness is determined by governance acceptance, not volume history.

### Embedded Partner Activation Steps

| Action | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Co-deliver first engagement | Signäl + partner run one SA together. Joint advisory session. Establish co-delivery cadence. |
| Define partner rate | Published price minus partner discount |
| Develop co-delivery playbook | How evidence intake works when partner manages the client relationship. Who schedules what. How the advisory session flows with two parties. |
| Formalize after first co-delivery | Terms, rates, governance boundaries, branding guidelines |
| Track partner-sourced vs direct pipeline | Understand channel mix as engagements accumulate |

## Channel Economics Impact on Pricing

The partner channel does not change the pricing strategy. It extends it.

| SKU | Direct | Referral Partner | Embedded Partner |
|-----|--------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **SA** | Published price | Published price (referral fee from Signäl margin) | Partner rate (Signäl receives discounted fee) |
| **SA-DD** | Published price | Published price (referral fee from Signäl margin) | Partner rate (Signäl receives discounted fee) |
| **SC** | Direct pricing | Customer converts direct (referral fee on first year) | Direct pricing. Partner may continue as co-delivery advisor. |
| **SE** | Direct pricing | Direct pricing (referral fee on first year) | Direct pricing. Alliance terms if applicable. |

**Key principle:** SC and SE are direct Signäl relationships. Partners bring the first engagement (SA/SA-DD). Expansion and platform relationships are direct. This is not commercial protectionism — it is a governance requirement:

- **Governance authority:** Continuous and enterprise intelligence involve ongoing qualification state evolution, SQO progression, and governance boundary enforcement. These require direct Signäl accountability.
- **Platform operation:** LENS provisioning, tenant management, RBAC, and operator authority workflows are Signäl-operated infrastructure. No partner operates this substrate.
- **Runtime responsibility:** Assessment runs, pipeline evolution, and intelligence updates are continuous Signäl obligations. Partner intermediation adds latency and governance risk.
- **Intelligence evolution:** New Domain Modules, Cognition Object updates, and pipeline improvements are deployed directly. The customer's intelligence surface must evolve with the platform.

Partners remain commercially involved where appropriate — co-delivering quarterly advisory sessions, maintaining the advisory relationship, providing transformation context. Direct Signäl ownership of SC/SE does not mean partner exclusion from the customer relationship. It means Signäl is accountable for the intelligence and the platform.

**Exception:** Strategic Alliance partners with volume commitments may bundle SC at partner rate. SE remains direct in all cases.
