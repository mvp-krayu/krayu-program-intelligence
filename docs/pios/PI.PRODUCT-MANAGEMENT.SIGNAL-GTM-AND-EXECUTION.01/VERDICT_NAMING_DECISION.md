# Verdict Naming Decision

> **Purpose:** Stress-test 4 finalist names against 4 buyer personas. Select one. Lock it.

---

## Candidates

1. **Structural Verdict**
2. **Signäl Verdict**
3. **Structural Dossier**
4. **Structural Intelligence Brief**

## Buyer Personas

- **CTO:** Technical leader. Values precision. Allergic to consulting jargon. Wants structural proof, not advice.
- **Board Member:** Non-technical. Wants a definitive document with authority. Values brevity, clarity, and institutional weight.
- **PE Due Diligence Lead:** Deal-focused. Needs an artifact for the investment committee package. Values speed, rigor, and defensibility.
- **Transformation Executive:** Program-focused. Needs structural evidence to support modernization decisions. Values actionability and credibility with engineering teams.

## Decision

### **Structural Verdict** — LOCKED

The 9-chapter governed document is canonically named **Structural Verdict** in all external-facing material.

**Substitution rules:**
- Internal architecture may retain "EIR" for lineage tracking
- All customer-facing material: "Structural Verdict"
- In LENS UI: "Structural Verdict" (not "EIR" or "Executive Intelligence Report")
- On invoices: "includes Structural Verdict"
- On landing pages: "Signäl Structural Verdict"
- In sales conversations: "Here's your Structural Verdict"

**Future evolution path:** When the brand "Signäl" is market-established (Year 3+), consider shortening to "Signäl Verdict" — dropping "Structural" and letting the brand carry the meaning.
