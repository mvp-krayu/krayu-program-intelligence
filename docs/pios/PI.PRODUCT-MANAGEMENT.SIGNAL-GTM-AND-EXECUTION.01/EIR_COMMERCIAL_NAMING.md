# EIR Commercial Naming — Replacement Candidates

> **Problem:** "Executive Intelligence Report" (EIR) is internal architecture language. "Report" sounds like consulting output. "Executive Intelligence" sounds like a government agency. Neither is board-grade premium product language.

> **Requirements:** Board-ready. Premium. Executive. Memorable. Defensible. Not consultancy-sounding. Not generic AI naming. Must work as: a deliverable name ("Here's your ___"), a product surface ("The ___ shows..."), and a landing page anchor ("Signäl ___").

---

## 10 Candidates

### 1. Structural Verdict

**Positioning:** The system examines structural evidence and delivers a verdict. Not an opinion. Not a recommendation. A governed structural determination.

- **Landing page:** "Signäl Structural Verdict — The governed structural intelligence artifact for executive leadership."
- **Sales:** "Here's your Structural Verdict. Nine chapters of named structural findings, each traceable to evidence."
- **In conversation:** "The Verdict shows three structural gravity centers driving your operational ceiling."

**Strengths:** Authoritative. Implies finality and evidence-based judgment. Distinctive — no competitor uses this language. "Verdict" carries weight without being prescriptive (a verdict states what IS, not what to DO). Works as both noun and concept ("What's the verdict?").

**Risks:** Could imply judgment/blame in sensitive contexts. Mitigation: always pair with "structural" — the verdict is about structure, not people.

---

### 2. Structural Intelligence Brief

**Positioning:** A premium briefing artifact — the kind of document that goes to a board or investment committee.

- **Landing page:** "Signäl Structural Intelligence Brief — Board-grade structural intelligence for executive decision-making."
- **Sales:** "Your Intelligence Brief covers nine areas of structural analysis, from topology to operational ceiling."
- **In conversation:** "The Brief identified dependency amplification as your primary execution constraint."

**Strengths:** "Brief" is executive language (intelligence briefing, board brief). Premium without being pretentious. Familiar format concept.

**Risks:** "Brief" can imply short/summary, which undersells a 9-chapter document. "Intelligence Brief" is used in government/military contexts — could feel heavy.

---

### 3. Structural Dossier

**Positioning:** A comprehensive intelligence file on your program's structural reality. Thorough. Definitive. Premium.

- **Landing page:** "Signäl Structural Dossier — The definitive structural intelligence artifact."
- **Sales:** "Your Structural Dossier contains nine chapters of governed findings."
- **In conversation:** "The Dossier revealed an operational ceiling driven by compound convergence."

**Strengths:** "Dossier" implies completeness and authority. Premium feel. Distinctive. No competitor uses it.

**Risks:** Can sound surveillance-oriented or adversarial. Works better for due diligence (SA-DD) than for CTO engagement (SA-1).

### 4. Structural Verdict Document

**Positioning:** Explicit document naming — premium, but clearly a delivered artifact.

- **Landing page:** "Signäl Structural Verdict Document"
- **Sales:** "Here's your Verdict Document."

**Strengths:** Unambiguous.

**Risks:** "Document" is generic. Loses the premium of just "Structural Verdict." Rejected — adding "Document" dilutes.

### 5. Execution Profile

**Positioning:** A structural profile of program execution capability — like a financial profile, but for structural execution reality.

- **Landing page:** "Signäl Execution Profile — See the structural reality behind your program's execution."
- **Sales:** "Your Execution Profile maps nine dimensions of structural intelligence."
- **In conversation:** "The Profile shows your execution topology has three concentration anchors."

**Strengths:** "Profile" is familiar (credit profile, risk profile). Executive-grade. Implies depth without intimidation. Works naturally in conversation.

**Risks:** Could sound like performance profiling (DevOps adjacent). Doesn't carry the weight of "Verdict."

### 6. Structural Compass

**Positioning:** Directional intelligence — shows where you are structurally and what constrains movement.

- **Landing page:** "Signäl Structural Compass — Navigate your program's structural reality."

- **In conversation:** "The Compass identified coupling inertia in your service layer."

**Strengths:** Evocative. Implies navigation and direction. Premium but approachable.

**Risks:** "Compass" implies direction/recommendation, which conflicts with "we observe, not prescribe." Could feel metaphorical rather than authoritative.

### 7. Structural Mosaic

**Positioning:** The complete picture assembled from structural evidence — each piece is evidence, the whole is intelligence.

- **Landing page:** "Signäl Structural Mosaic — The complete structural intelligence picture."
- **Sales:** "Your Mosaic assembles nine dimensions of structural intelligence."

**Strengths:** Distinctive. Visual metaphor.

**Risks:** Too abstract. Doesn't convey authority or finality. A mosaic is decorative — a verdict is authoritative.

### 8. Structural Lens

**Positioning:** The definitive view through the structural intelligence lens.

- **Landing page:** "Your Signäl Structural Lens"

**Strengths:** Connects to the LENS brand.

**Risks:** Creates brand confusion — LENS is the interactive surface, not the document. Using "Lens" for both collapses the distinction. Rejected.

---

### 9. Structural Mandate

**Positioning:** An authoritative structural determination that carries executive weight.

- **Landing page:** "Signäl Structural Mandate — What the structure demands."
- **Sales:** "Your Structural Mandate identifies the non-negotiable structural realities."
- **In conversation:** "The Mandate shows your operational ceiling is structurally reinforced."

**Strengths:** Executive weight. Authority. Implies action without prescribing specific actions.

**Risks:** "Mandate" implies obligation/command. Could feel heavy-handed. A mandate tells you what to do — Signäl tells you what IS.

---

### 10. Signäl Verdict

**Positioning:** Drop "Structural" from the name. The product IS structural. The Verdict IS from Signäl. Let the brand carry the structural identity.

- **Landing page:** "Your Signäl Verdict — Governed structural intelligence for executive leadership."
- **Sales:** "Here's your Verdict. Nine chapters. Named findings. Every one traceable to evidence."
- **In conversation:** "The Verdict found three structural conditions converging at Service Layer."

**Strengths:** Clean. Premium. Brand-reinforcing. "Signäl Verdict" is immediately distinctive and memorable. Short. Works in every context: sales call, email subject, board package cover page, landing page headline.

**Risks:** Without "Structural," new audiences may not immediately understand what's being verdicted. Mitigation: the product context always provides this — by the time someone receives a Verdict, they know what Signäl does.

## Recommendation

### **Structural Verdict**

Primary recommendation: **Structural Verdict**

| Criterion | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| Board-ready | YES — "The Structural Verdict shows..." is natural board language |
| Premium | YES — a verdict implies evidence, deliberation, and authority |
| Executive | YES — verdicts are executive-level determinations |
| Memorable | YES — distinctive, no competitor uses this framing |
| Defensible | YES — grounded in the product's core proposition (governed structural determination) |
| Not consultancy | YES — consultants produce "reports" and "assessments." A verdict is product output. |
| Not generic AI | YES — AI tools produce "insights" and "summaries." A verdict is structural determination. |
| Landing page | "Signäl Structural Verdict" — works as headline, URL slug, product name |
| Sales | "Here's your Structural Verdict" — natural, authoritative, premium |
| In-product | "Structural Verdict: 9 chapters, 17 findings" — clear deliverable label |

**Why "Verdict" works:**
- A verdict is the conclusion reached after examining evidence. That IS what the product does.
- A verdict states what IS true, not what to DO. This aligns with the product's non-prescriptive positioning.
- A verdict carries institutional weight — it's the kind of word used in contexts where truth matters.
- "Structural Verdict" is unique in the market. No competitor owns this language.

**Naming substitution:**
- Internal: EIR → Structural Verdict (architecture references may retain EIR for lineage)
- External: "Structural Verdict" in all customer-facing material, sales collateral, product surfaces
- In LENS UI: "Generate Structural Verdict" / "Your Structural Verdict is ready"
- On invoice: included in SKU (e.g., "SA-1: Signäl Structural Assessment — includes Structural Verdict")

**Alternative if shorter brand-led name preferred:** Signäl Verdict (candidate #10). Drops "Structural" and lets the brand carry the meaning. Consider for mature-stage branding when the market knows what Signäl means.
