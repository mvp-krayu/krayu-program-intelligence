# Signäl Canonical Demo — 20 Minutes

> **Purpose:** The canonical demonstration choreography. Every first demo follows this script. The demo creates "I need this" — not "interesting architecture."

> **Rule:** Start with the commercial wedge. End with the commercial close. Architecture is never mentioned. The product speaks.

---

## Pre-Demo Setup

**LENS loaded on:** Reference program (or customer program if available)

**Starting view:** BOARDROOM cognitive mode — the executive projection. The audience sees a clean, governed surface. Not a code tool. Not a dashboard. A structural intelligence instrument.

**Audience assumption:** CTO, VP Engineering, or technical executive. They have felt structural pain. They cannot prove it. They are skeptical of another tool.

---

## Act 1 — The Hook (Minutes 0-5)

### Beat 1: The Finding (0:00 - 1:30)

**Do NOT start with:**
- "Let me show you our platform..."
- "This is LENS, our structural intelligence surface..."
- "First, let me explain the topology..."

**START with a finding.**

> "Before I show you anything else — this is what we found on a real program. 847 files. 4,300 import dependencies. 17 structural domains."

Point to the Software Intelligence finding in BOARDROOM view:

> **"Dependency Amplification at HIGH severity in Service Layer."**

Pause. Let it land.

> "This means the true cost of changes to this component is systematically underestimated. Every modification cascades to dependent structures in ways your sprint planning doesn't account for. You feel this every cycle — a 'small change' takes three times longer than estimated."

**Why this works:** Every CTO has experienced this. They recognize the finding before you explain it. Recognition creates trust. Trust creates demand.

### Beat 2: The Proof (1:30 - 3:00)

> "This isn't an opinion. This isn't AI guessing. Watch."

Show the evidence trail — the finding traces to structural evidence:

> "Same evidence, same finding. Every time. Deterministic. If you ran this tomorrow on the same codebase, you would get the same finding at the same severity in the same location."

> "No other tool on the market can produce this finding. SonarQube sees code quality. Jira sees planned work. Neither sees structural execution consequences."

### Beat 3: The Second Finding (3:00 - 5:00)

Show a second Software Intelligence finding — choose the one most relevant to the audience:

**For CTO/VP Eng (modernization context):**
> "Coupling Inertia at ELEVATED severity — coupling patterns that actively resist architectural evolution. This is why your modernization stalls in certain areas. The structure itself resists change."

**For PE/M&A:**
> "Operational Ceiling present — structurally reinforced, driven by 4 converging conditions. This limits scaling potential. Adding headcount will not raise this ceiling. Only architectural intervention will."

**For Transformation Director:**
> "Compound Convergence — 3 independent structural conditions reinforcing each other at the same point. This isn't one problem. It's three problems compounding. Fixing one without the others won't resolve the systemic risk."

**Transition:** "Let me show you where this comes from."

## Act 2 — The Topology (Minutes 5-10)

### Beat 4: The Structural Map (5:00 - 7:00)

Switch to the structural execution topology view.

> "This is your program's structural execution topology. Not an org chart. Not a code dependency graph. The actual architecture of how your program executes."

Walk through the topology:

> "Each region is a structural domain. The edges are dependency relationships. The size and shading indicate structural mass and pressure concentration."

Point to the pressure zones:

> "See these highlighted regions? That's where structural pressure concentrates. The finding we just saw — Dependency Amplification in Service Layer — lives here."

> "This topology is derived from your execution evidence. It's not a diagram someone drew. It's the structural reality reconstructed from your program."

### Beat 5: The Consequence Chain (7:00 - 9:00)

Walk one consequence chain end-to-end:

> "Follow this dependency path. A change here propagates through these edges to these dependent structures. Your impact analysis in a pull request sees the first hop. Signäl sees the full propagation chain."

> "This is why a 'small change' in Service Layer costs 3x what you estimated. The structural blast radius is larger than it appears. Your planning tools can't show you this because they don't model structural propagation."

### Beat 6: The Operational Posture (9:00 - 10:00)

Show the structural posture assessment:

> "Your program's structural posture is INVESTIGATE — meaning structural conditions are present that warrant attention. Not a red light. Not a green light. A governed assessment with evidence."

> "This posture is qualified — it carries a confidence envelope, a governance boundary, and an evidence lineage. It's not a score. It's a structural assessment."

## Act 3 — The Deliverable (Minutes 10-15)

### Beat 7: The Structural Verdict (10:00 - 12:30)

> "Everything you've just seen — the findings, the topology, the consequence chains, the posture — is synthesized into this."

Show the Structural Verdict (9-chapter document):

> "This is the Structural Verdict. 9 chapters. Each chapter contains named structural findings. Each finding has four fields: what the structure shows, why it matters, the operational implication, and the leadership implication."

Walk through one finding in the Verdict:

> "Chapter 7 — Operational Ceiling. 'An operational ceiling is present, structurally reinforced by 4 conditions. This ceiling constrains execution velocity independent of staffing decisions. Resolution requires architectural intervention at the identified structural anchors.'"

> "This is the artifact you hand to your board. Or your investment committee. Or your architecture review board. It's governed, evidence-traced, and reproducible."

Highlight Chapter 8:

> "Chapter 8: 'What Traditional Analysis Cannot Discover.' This is the chapter that doesn't exist anywhere else. These are findings that no code review, no architecture audit, no consultant assessment can produce. Because they require structural topology reconstruction."

### Beat 8: The Evidence Record (12:30 - 13:30)

Show the Evidence Record:

> "The Evidence Record is your proof artifact. Structural posture, confidence envelope, topology capture, governance boundary. Auditable. Archivable."

> "If someone asks 'how do you know this?' — this is the answer. Every finding traces here."

### Beat 9: LENS Exploration (13:30 - 15:00)

Briefly show LENS depth — switch from BOARDROOM to BALANCED:

> "What you've seen is the executive projection — calibrated for leadership. The same structural truth is available at operational depth."

Show BALANCED view briefly:

> "At this level, your VP of Engineering sees the operational dynamics — why the structure produces this outcome, which coupling paths drive the inertia, how propagation creates the amplification effect."

> "Same truth. Different cognitive depth. Calibrated for the audience."

## Act 4 — The Close (Minutes 15-20)

### Beat 10: The Differentiation (15:00 - 17:00)

> "Let me be direct about what this is and what it isn't."

> "This is not a code quality tool. SonarQube measures your code. We reveal what your code structure means for execution. Different question. Different answer."

> "This is not an AI insight generator. Every finding is derived deterministically. Same input, same output. The intelligence is computed, not generated."

> "This is not a dashboard. Dashboards display data. Signäl reconstructs structural reality and derives consequences from it."

> "This is Structural Intelligence. A category that did not exist until now. And it's the reason you've been feeling structural pain you couldn't prove."

### Beat 11: The Offer (17:00 - 19:00)

> "Here's what I'm proposing."

> "We run a Structural Assessment on your program. You provide repository access — read-only. We ingest the evidence, run the structural intelligence pipeline, and deliver:"

> "LENS access — so you can explore your structural reality interactively."
> "A 9-chapter Structural Verdict — so you have a governed artifact for your board."
> "An Evidence Record — so you have auditable structural proof."
> "An advisory session — so we walk through every finding together."

> "Delivery: 3-5 business days from evidence intake."

### Beat 12: The Question (19:00 - 20:00)

> "You've been feeling this pain. You've been saying 'adding more developers won't help' and 'our modernization is stalling' and 'small changes keep causing big problems.' Now you've seen the structural cause."

> "Want to see this on your program?"

**Stop talking. Wait for the answer.**

## Demo Anti-Patterns

**NEVER do these:**

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails |
|-------------|-------------|
| Start with "Let me explain our architecture" | Nobody buys architecture. They buy outcomes. |
| Show topology first | Topology without a finding is a pretty picture. The finding creates demand for the topology. |
| Explain cognitive modes | Modes are a feature. The finding is the product. |
| Walk through all 9 consequence types | Pick the 2 most relevant. Depth beats breadth. |
| Compare to competitors | The demo is the differentiation. Naming competitors gives them oxygen. |
| Show the SQO qualification workflow | Internal governance. Not a demo moment. |
| Demonstrate investigation queries | Save for follow-up. The first demo sells the assessment, not the platform. |
| Say "powered by AI" | The moat is deterministic derivation, not AI. |
| Use internal terminology (PICP, PRE, CIP, L4, 75.x) | Instant credibility loss. |
| Rush past the first finding | The first finding IS the demo. If they don't feel it, nothing else matters. |

> "Coupling Inertia at ELEVATED severity. These coupling patterns actively resist the architectural evolution you're planning. Here's exactly where and through which coupling paths."

> "Execution Constriction — structural bottlenecks constrain throughput. Adding developers creates more coordination load at these bottlenecks without increasing flow capacity. This is why your velocity curve flattened."

## Timing Discipline

| Act | Minutes | Purpose |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Act 1 — The Hook | 0-5 | Create "I need this" with findings |
| Act 2 — The Topology | 5-10 | Show where and why |
| Act 3 — The Deliverable | 10-15 | Show what they receive |
| Act 4 — The Close | 15-20 | Differentiate + offer + ask |

**If you're running long:** Cut Beat 6 (posture) and Beat 8 (Evidence Record). Never cut Beat 1 (first finding) or Beat 12 (the question).

**If the audience asks questions during Act 1:** Answer them. Questions during the hook mean the hook is working. Let the conversation flow. Resume the choreography where it makes sense.

**If the audience is technical:** Spend more time in Act 2 (topology). They want to see the structural mechanics.

**If the audience is executive:** Spend more time in Act 3 (Structural Verdict). They want to see the deliverable they'll present to their board.
