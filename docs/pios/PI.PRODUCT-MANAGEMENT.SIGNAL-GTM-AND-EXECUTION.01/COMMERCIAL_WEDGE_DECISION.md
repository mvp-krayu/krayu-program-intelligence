# Commercial Wedge Decision

> **Purpose:** Challenge the assumption that SA-1 is the primary entry SKU. Assess all three Tier 1 SKUs against commercial reality. Select primary wedge, secondary, and expansion path.

---

## Candidates

| SKU | Name | Description |
|-----|------|-------------|
| **SA-1** | Signäl Structural Assessment | Structural topology + conditions + consequences. BOARDROOM + BALANCED. Structural Verdict + Evidence Record. |
| **SA-2** | Signäl Structural Assessment + Software Intelligence | SA-1 + SW-INTEL behavioral consequence module (9-slice). Full consequence posture. |
| **SA-DD** | Signäl Structural Due Diligence | SA-2 + full LENS access (4 modes + Investigation Protocol). For PE/M&A deal timelines. |

---

## Assessment

### Dimension 1: Ease of Explanation

| SKU | Assessment | Score |
|-----|-----------|-------|
| **SA-1** | "We show you the structural execution reality of your program." Clear, but abstract until demonstrated. The CTO asks: "What do you mean by structural?" Needs the demo. | 6/10 |
| **SA-2** | "We show you what your code structure means for execution — fragility, coupling inertia, dependency amplification. Structural consequences no other tool reveals." Concrete. The SW-INTEL findings ARE the explanation. A CTO hears "coupling inertia" and immediately recognizes their reality. | 9/10 |
| **SA-DD** | "Structural risk assessment for your acquisition target. 9-chapter Structural Verdict in 5 days." Crystal clear to the DD buyer. But requires a specific buyer (PE/M&A) — can't explain this to a general CTO. | 8/10 (for DD buyer), 3/10 (general) |

**Winner: SA-2.** SW-INTEL findings are self-explaining. "Execution Fragility at HIGH in Service Layer" needs zero context. SA-1 is abstract without the consequence layer. SA-DD is clear but audience-restricted.

### Dimension 2: Time-to-Value

| SKU | Assessment | Score |
|-----|-----------|-------|
| **SA-1** | Customer sees topology + conditions. Value is structural visibility. But without SW-INTEL consequences, the findings are "you have structural conditions" without "here's what they MEAN for execution." Value requires interpretation by the advisory team. | 6/10 |
| **SA-2** | Customer sees topology + conditions + consequences. Value is immediately operational: "Dependency Amplification means your changes cost more than you think." The consequence language IS the value. No advisory interpretation needed — the product speaks. | 9/10 |
| **SA-DD** | Same as SA-2 but with full LENS depth. For DD, time-to-value is measured in deal timelines — 5 days vs 4 weeks of manual DD. Extreme time-to-value for the right buyer. | 9/10 (for DD buyer) |

**Winner: SA-2.** The consequence layer IS the time-to-value accelerator. Without it, the customer sees structure but doesn't get operational meaning. With it, the product self-delivers value.

### Dimension 3: Buyer Urgency

| SKU | Assessment | Score |
|-----|-----------|-------|
| **SA-1** | Moderate urgency. "I want to understand our structural reality" is a strategic desire, not a burning need. No deadline forcing the purchase. | 5/10 |
| **SA-2** | Higher urgency. "Our modernization is stalling and I can't figure out why" / "We keep having unexpected regressions" / "We're scaling and things are breaking." SW-INTEL findings address pain that CTOs experience NOW. | 7/10 |
| **SA-DD** | Maximum urgency. Deal timelines. "We're closing in 6 weeks and I need structural risk assessment." The deadline is external and immovable. But this buyer is episodic (deal-driven), not recurring. | 10/10 (when active) |

**Winner: SA-DD on urgency. SA-2 on frequency.** DD has the highest urgency but lowest frequency. SA-2 has persistent urgency — CTOs always have structural pain. SA-1 has "nice to have" urgency.

### Dimension 4: Pricing Power

| SKU | Assessment | Score |
|-----|-----------|-------|
| **SA-1** | Moderate. Structural topology is novel but hard to price against alternatives (because there ARE no alternatives — which means no price anchor). Risk of "interesting but how much is that worth?" | 5/10 |
| **SA-2** | High. SW-INTEL findings have direct cost avoidance implications: "Dependency Amplification means every Service Layer change costs 3x what you estimated." The pricing conversation becomes: "What's it worth to know that BEFORE you commit the sprint?" | 8/10 |
| **SA-DD** | Maximum. DD has deal-driven pricing power. "What's 5 days of structural intelligence worth compared to 4 weeks of manual DD at $50K+?" The alternative is expensive consulting. Direct substitution pricing. | 10/10 |

**Winner: SA-DD on absolute pricing power. SA-2 on justifiable recurring pricing.** DD commands premium because the alternative (manual DD consulting) is expensive and slow. SA-2 justifies pricing through operational cost avoidance.

### Dimension 5: Demonstrability

| SKU | Assessment | Score |
|-----|-----------|-------|
| **SA-1** | Demonstrable — show topology. But the demo peaks at "here's your structure" and the audience thinks "OK, now what?" The demo needs the consequence layer to create the "holy shit" moment. | 5/10 |
| **SA-2** | Highly demonstrable. The demo sequence is: topology (context) → SW-INTEL finding (hook) → consequence (impact) → Structural Verdict (deliverable). This IS the demo. SW-INTEL findings are the emotional peak. | 10/10 |
| **SA-DD** | Same demo as SA-2, but with the DD framing: "This is what we'd deliver as part of your due diligence." The demo is identical — the framing is different. | 9/10 |

**Winner: SA-2.** The demo IS SA-2. Without SW-INTEL, the demo has no climax. The topology is context; the consequence findings are the payoff.

### Dimension 6: Conversion Likelihood

| SKU | Assessment | Score |
|-----|-----------|-------|
| **SA-1** | Lower conversion. The value proposition ("see your structure") is novel but not urgent. The CTO thinks: "Interesting. Let me think about it." No forcing function. | 5/10 |
| **SA-2** | Higher conversion. The findings create immediate recognition: "YES, that's exactly what we're experiencing." Recognition drives commitment. The CTO doesn't need to think — they've already felt this pain. They want the full picture. | 8/10 |
| **SA-DD** | Highest conversion (within the buyer set). The deal timeline IS the forcing function. But the buyer set is small and episodic. | 9/10 (for DD buyers), N/A (general) |

**Winner: SA-2 for volume. SA-DD for conversion rate within its niche.**

## Decision

### SA-1 does NOT win as primary wedge.

SA-1 without Software Intelligence is structural topology without operational meaning. The demo has no climax. The value proposition is "see your structure" without "here's what it means." It's a harder sell because the buyer can't self-justify the ROI — they need the advisory team to interpret the findings.

### The primary wedge depends on go-to-market motion:

```
PRIMARY WEDGE           SA-2 (Structural Assessment + Software Intelligence)

                        This is the product you lead with in every
                        first conversation, every demo, every landing
                        page. SW-INTEL consequences are the hook.
                        The demo IS SA-2. The "holy shit" IS SA-2.

                        SA-1 is retired as a standalone offering.
                        There is no version of the product without
                        Software Intelligence.

SECONDARY WEDGE         SA-DD (Structural Due Diligence)

                        Specialized packaging of SA-2 for PE/M&A.
                        Same intelligence, different framing and
                        urgency. Premium pricing. Deal-timeline delivery.
                        Marketed separately to DD buyers.

EXPANSION PATH          SA-2 → SC-1 (Continuous)

                        After the first assessment, the CTO wants
                        ongoing visibility. SA-2 converts to SC-1.
                        The expansion conversation: "Want to track
                        how this evolves?"
```

### Critical implication: SA-1 is eliminated.

SA-1 (without SW-INTEL) is not a viable standalone SKU. The evidence:
- The demo doesn't work without SW-INTEL findings
- The value proposition is incomplete without consequence analysis
- The buyer cannot self-justify ROI without operational meaning
- SW-INTEL is the commercial hook — selling without the hook is selling without the product

**New SKU structure:**

| SKU | Name | Position |
|-----|------|----------|
| ~~SA-1~~ | ~~Structural Assessment (without SW-INTEL)~~ | **ELIMINATED** |
| **SA** | **Signäl Structural Assessment** | PRIMARY WEDGE — includes Software Intelligence by default |
| **SA-DD** | **Signäl Structural Due Diligence** | SECONDARY WEDGE — SA repackaged for PE/M&A |
| **SC** | **Signäl Continuous Intelligence** | EXPANSION — ongoing access |
| **SE** | **Signäl Enterprise Intelligence** | PLATFORM — enterprise license |

Software Intelligence is not an add-on. It IS the product. The teaser/full toggle remains for upsell within LENS (Tier 2+), but the entry SKU always includes full SW-INTEL activation.
